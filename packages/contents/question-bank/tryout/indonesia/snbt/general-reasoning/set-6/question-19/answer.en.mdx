To find the **inconsistent** statement, we simply need to observe the mismatched trends between two key variables: **Rice Production** and **Rice Procurement**.

Observe the data from **<InlineMath math="2002" /> to <InlineMath math="2004" />**:
- **Rice Production** experienced an **increase** (<InlineMath math="33.47 \to 35.32\text{ million}" /> tons).
- **Rice Procurement** actually experienced a **decrease** (<InlineMath math="35.28 \to 33.69\text{ million}" /> tons).

This fact indicates an **inverse relationship**. When production reached its highest point, procurement hit its lowest.

Therefore, the statement that *"high rice production is consistent with high rice procurement"* directly contradicts the table data.
