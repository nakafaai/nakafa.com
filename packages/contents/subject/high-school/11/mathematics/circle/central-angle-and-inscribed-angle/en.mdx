A central angle is an angle formed by two radii of a circle with the vertex located at the center of the circle. The sides of the central angle are radii that connect the center to points on the circle.

## Definition of Inscribed Angle

An inscribed angle is an angle formed by two chords with the vertex located on the circle. The sides of the inscribed angle are chords that connect the vertex to two other points on the circle.

### Theorem of Central Angle and Inscribed Angle Relationship

<BlockMath math="\text{Inscribed angle} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{Central angle}" />

If a central angle and an inscribed angle subtend the same arc, then:

- <InlineMath math="\text{measure of inscribed angle} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{measure of central angle}" />
- <InlineMath math="\text{measure of central angle} = 2 \times \text{measure of inscribed angle}" />

**Proof steps:**

1. Construct line <InlineMath math="CD" /> that passes through point <InlineMath math="O" /> (center of the circle)
2. Note that <InlineMath math="OA = OB = OC = OD" /> (radii of the circle)
3. Triangles <InlineMath math="AOC" /> and <InlineMath math="BOC" /> are isosceles triangles
4. Let <InlineMath math="\angle ACO = x" /> and <InlineMath math="\angle BCO = y" />
5. Since they are isosceles triangles: <InlineMath math="\angle CAO = x" /> and <InlineMath math="\angle CBO = y" />
6. Exterior angles of triangles: <InlineMath math="\angle AOD = 2x" /> and <InlineMath math="\angle BOD = 2y" />
7. Therefore: <InlineMath math="\angle AOB = 2x + 2y = 2(x + y) = 2 \times \angle ACB" />

## Practice Problems

1. If the central angle of a circle is <InlineMath math="120^\circ" />, what is the measure of the inscribed angle that subtends the same arc?

2. Inscribed angle <InlineMath math="\angle ABC = 45^\circ" />. Find the measure of central angle <InlineMath math="\angle AOB" />!

3. In a circle, inscribed angle <InlineMath math="PQR" /> subtends a diameter. What is the measure of angle <InlineMath math="PQR" />?

4. Two inscribed angles subtend the same arc. If one angle measures <InlineMath math="25^\circ" />, find the measure of the other angle!

### Answer Key

1. Inscribed angle is <InlineMath math="\frac{1}{2} \times 120^\circ = 60^\circ" />

2. Central angle <InlineMath math="\angle AOB = 2 \times 45^\circ = 90^\circ" />

3. <InlineMath math="\angle PQR = 90^\circ" /> (an inscribed angle subtending a diameter is always <InlineMath math="90^\circ" />)

4. The other angle is <InlineMath math="25^\circ" /> (inscribed angles subtending the same arc have equal measures)
