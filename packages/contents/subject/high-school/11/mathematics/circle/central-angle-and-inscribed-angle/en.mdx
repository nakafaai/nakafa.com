A central angle is an angle formed by two radii of a circle with the vertex located at the center of the circle. The sides of the central angle are radii that connect the center to points on the circle.

### Theorem of Central Angle and Inscribed Angle Relationship

<BlockMath math="\text{Inscribed angle} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{Central angle}" />

If a central angle and an inscribed angle subtend the same arc, then:

- Measure of inscribed angle = <InlineMath math="\frac{1}{2}" /> × measure of central angle
- Measure of central angle = 2 × measure of inscribed angle

**Proof steps:**

1. Construct line CD that passes through point O (center of the circle)
2. Note that OA = OB = OC = OD (radii of the circle)
3. Triangles AOC and BOC are isosceles triangles
4. Let <InlineMath math="\angle ACO = x" /> and <InlineMath math="\angle BCO = y" />
5. Since they are isosceles triangles: <InlineMath math="\angle CAO = x" /> and <InlineMath math="\angle CBO = y" />
6. Exterior angles of triangles: <InlineMath math="\angle AOD = 2x" /> and <InlineMath math="\angle BOD = 2y" />
7. Therefore: <InlineMath math="\angle AOB = 2x + 2y = 2(x + y) = 2 \times \angle ACB" />

## Practice Problems

1. If the central angle of a circle is 120°, what is the measure of the inscribed angle that subtends the same arc?

2. Inscribed angle ABC = 45°. Find the measure of central angle AOB!

3. In a circle, inscribed angle PQR subtends a diameter. What is the measure of angle PQR?

4. Two inscribed angles subtend the same arc. If one angle measures 25°, find the measure of the other angle!

### Answer Key

1. Inscribed angle = <InlineMath math="\frac{1}{2} \times 120° = 60°" />

2. Central angle AOB = <InlineMath math="2 \times 45° = 90°" />

3. Angle PQR = 90° (inscribed angle subtending a diameter is always 90°)

4. The other angle = 25° (inscribed angles subtending the same arc have equal measures)
