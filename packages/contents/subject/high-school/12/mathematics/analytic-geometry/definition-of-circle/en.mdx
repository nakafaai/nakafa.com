## Understanding Circle

A circle is the collection of all points on a plane that have the same distance from one fixed point. This fixed point is called the **center of the circle**, while the equal distance from the center to every point on the circle is called the **radius**.

Imagine you tie a string to a nail, then pull the string tight and draw a complete curve around the nail. The curve that forms is what we call a circle, the nail is its center, and the length of the string is its radius.

## Mathematical Definition

Now, let's create a more formal definition. Mathematically, a circle with center <InlineMath math="P(a, b)" /> and radius <InlineMath math="r" /> is the set of all points <InlineMath math="(x, y)" /> that satisfy the condition:

<BlockMath math="d(P, (x,y)) = r" />

Where <InlineMath math="d(P, (x,y))" /> is the distance from the center point P to the point <InlineMath math="(x, y)" /> on the circle.

If we use the distance formula in the Cartesian coordinate system, we can write it like this:

<BlockMath math="\sqrt{(x-a)^2 + (y-b)^2} = r" />

## Circle Equation

From the mathematical definition above, we can derive the **circle equation** by squaring both sides:

<div className="flex flex-col gap-4">
  <BlockMath math="\sqrt{(x-a)^2 + (y-b)^2} = r" />
  <BlockMath math="(x-a)^2 + (y-b)^2 = r^2" />
</div>

This is the **general equation of a circle** with center <InlineMath math="(a, b)" /> and radius <InlineMath math="r" />. This formula is very useful for determining whether a point lies inside, outside, or exactly on the circle.

### Special Form of Circle Equation

There's one special case that's interesting. When the center of the circle is at the origin <InlineMath math="(0, 0)" />, the circle equation becomes simpler:

<BlockMath math="x^2 + y^2 = r^2" />

This form is very practical because it's easier to calculate and understand.

## Important Circle Elements

There are several important terms you need to understand:

1. **Center of circle** is the fixed point that serves as a reference for all points on the circle. All points on the circle have the same distance to this center.

2. **Radius** is the distance from the center of the circle to any point on the circle. In one circle, all radii have the same length.

3. **Diameter** is a straight line that connects two points on the circle and passes through the center. The length of the diameter is always twice the length of the radius, or <InlineMath math="d = 2r" />.

## Application Example

Now let's apply the circle definition to determine the circle equation.

**Example**: Determine the equation of a circle centered at <InlineMath math="(3, -2)" /> with radius 5.

**Solution**: 
We just need to use the general formula for circle equation with center <InlineMath math="(a, b)" /> and radius <InlineMath math="r" />:

<div className="flex flex-col gap-4">
  <BlockMath math="(x-a)^2 + (y-b)^2 = r^2" />
  <BlockMath math="(x-3)^2 + (y-(-2))^2 = 5^2" />
  <BlockMath math="(x-3)^2 + (y+2)^2 = 25" />
</div>

So the circle equation is <InlineMath math="(x-3)^2 + (y+2)^2 = 25" />.
