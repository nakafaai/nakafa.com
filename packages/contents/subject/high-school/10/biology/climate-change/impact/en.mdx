## Climate impacts spread through ecosystems

Climate change is not only hotter air. The Indonesian grade 10 science textbook discusses drought, fires, floods, agriculture, coral bleaching, sea turtle sex ratios, and disease risk. WHO connects climate change to health through extreme heat, air quality, water, food, and infectious disease in [Climate change and health](https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/climate-change-and-health).

## Impacts often move through cause-and-effect chains

Ocean heat can stress corals, weakened coral changes fish habitat, and coastal communities can be affected. Drought can stress crops, reduce yield, and disturb food availability. Because impacts cascade, solutions also need cross-sector work.

## Self-check

Why can ocean warming affect people who live far from coral reefs?

Answer: coral reefs connect to biodiversity, fisheries, coastal protection, and coastal economies.

Explanation: a change in one ecosystem can spread through food webs and human activities.
