## The Impact Enters the Web of Life

Climate impacts appear when temperature, water, seasons, and extreme events change enough that organisms must adapt, move, or face population decline.

IPCC AR6 WGII explains impacts on ecosystems, human health, food production, and infrastructure in [Impacts, Adaptation and Vulnerability](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg2/).

## Risk Is Uneven

Two places can warm by the same amount but face different risks. Areas with fewer adaptation resources are usually more vulnerable to floods, crop failure, or disease.

WHO discusses climate and health links, including extreme heat, air quality, and infectious disease, in [Climate change and health](https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/climate-change-and-health).

## Self-Check

Why are climate impacts not the same in every region?

Because risk depends on hazard exposure, vulnerability, and each region's capacity to adapt.
