## Climate problems need cross-country cooperation

Greenhouse gases mix in the atmosphere. Impacts also cross borders through oceans, air, food, migration, and economics. The Indonesian grade 10 science textbook discusses UNFCCC, IPCC, and the Paris Agreement, which Indonesia signed on April 22, 2016. IPCC summarizes the scientific basis and global response options in the [AR6 Synthesis Report](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/syr/).

## Cooperation must be measurable

Climate commitments are not enough if they stay as promises. They need targets, data, reporting, finance, technology, and evaluation. Because countries differ in responsibility and capacity, global cooperation also involves fairness: who reduces most, who needs adaptation support, and who faces the greatest risk?

## Self-check

Why can climate change not be solved by one country alone?

Answer: greenhouse gases mix in the atmosphere and impacts cross national borders.

Explanation: one country's action matters, but the global climate is shaped by total emissions and cooperation among many parties.
