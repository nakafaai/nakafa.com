## Mitigation and adaptation have different goals

Mitigation means reducing or stabilizing greenhouse gases. Adaptation means adjusting so climate impacts cause less harm. The Indonesian grade 10 science textbook gives examples such as forest restoration, plastic reduction, and drought-tolerant crops. IPCC discusses mitigation and adaptation as key responses in the [AR6 Synthesis Report](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/syr/).

## Both responses need to move together

Adaptation without mitigation means society keeps chasing stronger impacts. Mitigation without adaptation ignores impacts that are already happening. A coherent climate response asks two questions: how do we reduce the cause, and how do we protect people and ecosystems while change continues?

## Self-check

Is building a seawall mitigation or adaptation?

Answer: adaptation.

Explanation: a seawall does not directly reduce greenhouse gases, but it can reduce risk from coastal flooding or sea level rise.
