## Two Paths That Must Work Together

Mitigation means reducing the causes of climate change, especially greenhouse gas emissions. Adaptation means reducing impacts that are already happening or expected.

IPCC AR6 WGIII discusses mitigation options across energy, transport, industry, and land in [Mitigation of Climate Change](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg3/).

## Do Not Mix Them Up

Planting trees can be mitigation if it increases carbon storage. A flood barrier is adaptation because it reduces flood impact. Both matter, but they answer different parts of the problem.

UNEP explains the growing need for adaptation as climate risk increases in the [Adaptation Gap Report](https://www.unep.org/resources/adaptation-gap-report).

## Self-Check

Is building a flood early-warning system mitigation or adaptation?

Adaptation, because it reduces risk and losses from climate impacts, not greenhouse gas emissions directly.
