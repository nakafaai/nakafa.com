## Climate symptoms appear across many indicators

Climate is the long-term pattern of weather in an area. Climate change means long-term change in temperature and weather patterns. The Indonesian grade 10 science textbook highlights global average temperature rise, ocean warming, shrinking ice, and sea level rise. NASA summarizes similar evidence in [Climate Change Evidence](https://science.nasa.gov/climate-change/evidence/).

## Weather and climate are different

Today can be cooler than yesterday while the climate trend still warms. Weather describes current or short-term conditions. Climate describes long-term patterns. That is why climate data needs time series, not one daily experience.

## Self-check

Why is one heavy rain day not enough to conclude climate change?

Answer: climate change is read from long-term patterns, not one weather event.

Explanation: a single event must be compared with trends, frequency, intensity, and historical data.
