## Signals That Do Not Stand Alone

Climate change is a long-term change in temperature, rainfall, and atmospheric patterns. Its symptoms are not simply “today feels hot”, but repeated trends across many systems.

NASA summarizes major indicators such as global temperature rise, ocean warming, shrinking ice, and sea level rise in [Global Climate Change: Vital Signs](https://climate.nasa.gov/vital-signs/).

## Weather Is Not Climate

Weather changes hour by hour. Climate is the statistical pattern of weather over long periods. That is why climate evidence comes from data collections, not one event.

IPCC AR6 states that warming is visible across independent indicators in the atmosphere, ocean, cryosphere, and biosphere in the [Summary for Policymakers](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg1/).

## Self-Check

Why does one very cold day not disprove climate change?

Because climate change is read from long-term trends. A cold day is weather; climate is the pattern over many years.
