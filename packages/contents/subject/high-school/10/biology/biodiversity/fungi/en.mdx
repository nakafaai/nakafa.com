## Fungi absorb nutrients from the environment

Fungi are not plants. They are eukaryotic heterotrophs, meaning they obtain organic material from other organisms or remains. The Indonesian grade 10 science textbook explains hyphae, mycelium, chitin cell walls, and spores. OpenStax describes fungi as heterotrophs that absorb nutrients and act as decomposers in [Characteristics of Fungi](https://openstax.org/books/biology-2e/pages/24-1-characteristics-of-fungi).

## Fungal feeding differs from animal feeding

Animals usually digest food inside the body. Fungi release enzymes into the environment, break down large molecules, then absorb smaller molecules. This is why fungi are important decomposers. Without decomposers, nutrients would stay locked in remains for longer.

## Self-check

Why does mycelium help fungi obtain nutrients?

Answer: mycelium expands the surface area in contact with the substrate.

Explanation: more hyphal network means more area to release enzymes and absorb products of decomposition.
