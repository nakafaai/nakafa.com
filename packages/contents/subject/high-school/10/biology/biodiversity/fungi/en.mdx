## Fungi Are Not Plants

Fungi do not photosynthesize like plants. Many fungi get nutrients by absorbing organic matter from their surroundings. The body of many multicellular fungi is made of hyphae, thin threads that form a mycelium network.

OpenStax Biology 2e explains hyphae and fungal ecosystem roles in [Characteristics of Fungi](https://openstax.org/books/biology-2e/pages/24-1-characteristics-of-fungi).

## Decomposers That Return Matter

Fungi help decompose remains of living things. This role returns nutrients to soil so matter can be used again by other organisms.

## Self-Check

Why are fungi not classified as plants just because they stay in one place?

Because fungi do not photosynthesize and get nutrients by absorbing organic matter.
