## Classification helps us find patterns

Classification means grouping living things based on chosen traits. The Indonesian grade 10 science textbook distinguishes artificial, natural, and phylogenetic classification. OpenStax explains that modern systematics organizes life's diversity through evolutionary relationships in [Organizing Life on Earth](https://openstax.org/books/biology-2e/pages/20-1-organizing-life-on-earth).

## A group name needs a reason

Good classification is not only naming. It helps us predict traits. If two organisms are grouped because they are closely related, we can expect patterns in structure, physiology, or genes. That is why phylogenetic classification explains biological relationships better than grouping only by color, size, or habitat.

## Self-check

Why are bats not grouped with birds just because both can fly?

Answer: flight is one visible trait, not the strongest evidence of relatedness.

Explanation: good classification considers many traits and evolutionary relationships, not one practical trait.
