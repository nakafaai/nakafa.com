## Small Cells with Large Effects

Bacteria are prokaryotic organisms, meaning they do not have a membrane-bound nucleus. Their genetic material is in a nucleoid region, while ribosomes and cytoplasm sit inside the cell membrane.

OpenStax Biology 2e explains prokaryotic structure and shape variation in [Prokaryotic Cells](https://openstax.org/books/biology-2e/pages/4-2-prokaryotic-cells).

## Bacteria Are Not Always Enemies

Some bacteria cause disease, but many bacteria support decomposition, fermented food production, and microbiome balance.

## Self-Check

What is the main difference between bacteria and animal cells when you look at the nucleus?

Bacteria do not have a membrane-bound nucleus, while animal cells do.
