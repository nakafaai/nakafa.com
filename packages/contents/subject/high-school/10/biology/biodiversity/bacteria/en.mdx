## Bacteria are prokaryotic cells

Bacteria are unicellular prokaryotes. Prokaryotic means their cells do not have a membrane-bound nucleus. The Indonesian grade 10 science textbook emphasizes bacterial shapes such as coccus, bacillus, and spiral forms, plus structures such as cell wall, membrane, cytoplasm, ribosomes, and nucleoid. OpenStax explains common prokaryotic shapes and structures in [Structure of Prokaryotes: Bacteria and Archaea](https://openstax.org/books/biology-2e/pages/22-2-structure-of-prokaryotes-bacteria-and-archaea).

## Bacteria are not always harmful

Some bacteria cause disease, but many are useful. The Indonesian grade 10 science textbook gives examples in food, biotechnology, vitamins, and bioremediation. So the biological question is not "good or bad bacteria" but: which species, where does it live, and what does it interact with?

## Self-check

What is the key difference between a bacterial nucleoid and an animal cell nucleus?

Answer: the nucleoid is not enclosed by a nuclear membrane, while an animal cell nucleus is.

Explanation: bacteria are prokaryotic; animal cells are eukaryotic.
