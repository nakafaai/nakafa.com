## Diversity is read at three levels

Biodiversity means variation in life. The Indonesian grade 10 science textbook divides it into genetic, species, and ecosystem diversity. OpenStax also uses genetic, species, and ecosystem levels to explain biodiversity's value in [The Importance of Biodiversity to Human Life](https://openstax.org/books/biology-2e/pages/47-3-the-importance-of-biodiversity-to-human-life).

## One place can be rich at one level and poor at another

A garden may have many mango trees, but if they all come from the same variety, genetic variation is narrow. A forest can hold many organism types and many microhabitats at once. So the question "is biodiversity high or low" should always be followed by: at which level?

## Self-check

If one area has many banana varieties, but they are still bananas, which diversity level is most directly visible?

Answer: genetic diversity.

Explanation: the variation is inside one kind of organism, not among species or ecosystems.
