## Biodiversity Is More Than Many Species

Biodiversity means variation in life. That variation can be read at genetic, species, and ecosystem levels. Different rice varieties, many bird species, and mangrove forests that differ from rainforests are all biodiversity patterns.

OpenStax Biology 2e explains the value of biodiversity at genetic, species, and ecosystem levels in [The Importance of Biodiversity to Human Life](https://openstax.org/books/biology-2e/pages/47-3-the-importance-of-biodiversity-to-human-life).

## Why the Levels Are Separated

A garden may have many plants, but if they are all from one variety, genetic diversity is narrow. A forest can look similar from far away while still holding many microhabitats.

## Self-Check

If an area has many mango varieties but all are still one species, what diversity level is most directly visible?

Genetic diversity, because the variation is within one species.
