## Ecosystems Are Built from Relationships

An ecosystem is the relationship between living organisms and their physical environment. It includes producers, consumers, decomposers, energy flow, and matter cycling.

OpenStax Biology 2e explains energy flow and ecosystem structure in [Energy Flow through Ecosystems](https://openstax.org/books/biology-2e/pages/46-2-energy-flow-through-ecosystems).

## Energy Flows, Matter Cycles

Energy usually flows from producers to consumers and is lost as heat. Matter such as carbon, nitrogen, and minerals can cycle back through decomposition.

## Self-Check

Why are decomposers important even when they are not shown in a simple food chain?

Because decomposers return matter from organism remains to the environment so it can be used again.
