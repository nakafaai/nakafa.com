## Ecosystems work through energy flow

An ecosystem is the relationship between living organisms and their physical environment. The Indonesian grade 10 science textbook introduces organisms in ecosystems through energy flow and matter cycling. OpenStax explains that energy flows through trophic levels while matter is recycled in [Ecology of Ecosystems](https://openstax.org/books/biology-2e/pages/46-1-ecology-of-ecosystems).

## Relationships matter more than name lists

Memorizing producers and consumers is not enough. Ecosystems make sense when we see relationships: where energy enters, where matter returns, and what changes when one population shifts. That is why a food web is more realistic than a single straight food chain, even though a chain is a useful first step.

## Self-check

Why are decomposers important even though they are not always at the top of a food chain?

Answer: decomposers return matter from remains to the environment.

Explanation: energy flows through ecosystems, but matter must be recycled so nutrients can be used again by producers.
