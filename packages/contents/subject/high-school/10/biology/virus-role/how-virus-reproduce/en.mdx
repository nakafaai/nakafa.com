## Viruses replicate inside host cells

Viruses do not divide like bacteria. Viral replication means producing new virus particles by using the machinery of a host cell. The Indonesian grade 10 science textbook summarizes the stages as attachment, penetration, synthesis, assembly, and lysis or release. OpenStax also explains that many viruses attach to host receptors before entering and completing replication inside the cell in [Viral Morphology](https://openstax.org/books/biology-2e/pages/21-1-viral-evolution-morphology-and-classification).

## Lytic and lysogenic cycles differ by genome behavior

In the lytic cycle, production of new virions proceeds quickly until the cell is damaged or ruptured. In the lysogenic cycle, viral genetic material can remain with the host's genetic material before becoming active again. The key difference is not just speed. It is whether the viral genome immediately produces particles or waits inside the cell.

Read the process this way: viruses do not give birth. A host cell is temporarily turned into a production site.

## Self-check

Why can attachment determine which cell type a virus infects?

Answer: attachment depends on a match between viral proteins and receptors on the cell surface.

Explanation: without a matching receptor, entry is difficult and the later replication stages do not happen.
