## A Virus Does Not Divide Like a Cell

A virus does not split the way bacteria do. It reproduces by delivering genetic material into a host cell, then using the cell's machinery to build new viral parts.

OpenStax Biology 2e discusses lytic and lysogenic cycles as major patterns of viral reproduction in [Virus Infections and Hosts](https://openstax.org/books/biology-2e/pages/21-2-virus-infections-and-hosts).

## Lytic and Lysogenic Cycles

In the lytic cycle, viruses quickly make many copies and usually damage the host cell. In the lysogenic cycle, viral genetic material can become part of the host genome and be copied before becoming active again.

## Self-Check

Why must a virus enter a host cell before its number can increase?

Because a virus lacks the full machinery needed to make proteins and copy itself. That machinery is inside the host cell.
