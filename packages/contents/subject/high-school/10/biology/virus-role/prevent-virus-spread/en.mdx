## Prevention Means Breaking a Route

Viruses spread through specific routes, such as respiratory droplets, hand contact, contaminated food or water, blood, or vectors like mosquitoes. Prevention works when that route is interrupted.

CDC summarizes hand hygiene as an important way to prevent infection spread in [When and How to Wash Your Hands](https://www.cdc.gov/handwashing/when-how-handwashing.html). WHO explains vaccination as a way to train the immune system in [Vaccines and immunization](https://www.who.int/health-topics/vaccines-and-immunization).

## Match Prevention to the Route

Not every prevention step works for every virus in the same way. For airborne spread, ventilation and masks are more relevant. For vector-borne viruses, controlling mosquitoes or other carriers becomes important.

## Self-Check

Why can handwashing not replace vaccination?

Because handwashing reduces virus transfer outside the body, while vaccination trains immune response inside the body.
