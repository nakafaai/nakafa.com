## Prevention follows the transmission route

There is no single prevention step that fits every virus. The Indonesian grade 10 science textbook compares transmission through mosquitoes, skin contact, body fluids, surfaces, and droplets. CDC explains respiratory illness prevention through steps such as cleaner air, hand hygiene, masking when risk is high, and staying home when sick in [Preventing Respiratory Illnesses](https://www.cdc.gov/respiratory-viruses/prevention/index.html).

## Strategies must match the risk

Dengue prevention centers on the mosquito vector, such as reducing breeding sites. HIV is linked to body fluids, so its prevention differs from flu prevention. Respiratory viruses are often discussed through ventilation, masks, cough etiquette, and isolation while sick.

The rule is simple: find the virus exit point, the movement route, and the entry point into the next host. Strong prevention usually interrupts more than one point in that chain.

## Self-check

Why is handwashing not enough for every viral disease?

Answer: viruses spread through different transmission routes.

Explanation: handwashing helps with contact or surface routes, but viruses carried by mosquitoes, body fluids, or air need additional route-specific strategies.
