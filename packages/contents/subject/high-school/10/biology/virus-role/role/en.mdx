## A virus role depends on context

Viruses can cause disease, but that is not the whole biology of viruses. The Indonesian grade 10 science textbook includes viruses as disease agents and as biotechnology tools, including gene therapy vectors, vaccine development, and oncolytic viruses. OpenStax also emphasizes that viruses infect nearly all forms of life, from bacteria to eukaryotes, in [Viral Evolution, Morphology, and Classification](https://openstax.org/books/biology-2e/pages/21-1-viral-evolution-morphology-and-classification).

## One biological object can have different effects

A clean way to think is to separate mechanism from effect. The basic mechanism is cell entry and use of cellular systems. The effect can be disease, a shift in microbial populations, or a tool for research and therapy. This avoids two mistakes: treating every virus as only harmful, or ignoring infection risk.

At this level, read viral roles as context maps. Ask: which host is infected, what route is used, and what happens at the cell, organism, or ecosystem level?

## Self-check

Why can viruses cause disease and also serve as biotechnology tools?

Answer: the same cell-entry mechanism can damage cells in natural infection but can be controlled and redesigned to deliver genetic material.

Explanation: the context and risk control are different, even though both involve viral biology.
