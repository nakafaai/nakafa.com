## A Virus Has to Be Read in Context

Viruses can cause disease, but they also matter in ecosystems and research. Bacteriophages, viruses that infect bacteria, can influence bacterial populations in the environment.

NCBI Bookshelf describes bacteriophages as viruses that infect bacteria and have been widely used to study molecular biology in [Bacteriophages](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK493185/).

## Benefits Without Ignoring Risk

A virus can be harmful in one context and useful in research. The better question is not whether all viruses are good or bad, but which virus it is, what host it infects, and under what conditions.

## Self-Check

Why is a bacteriophage not automatically considered dangerous to humans?

Because a bacteriophage infects bacteria. Its effect on humans depends on the target bacteria and how it is used.
