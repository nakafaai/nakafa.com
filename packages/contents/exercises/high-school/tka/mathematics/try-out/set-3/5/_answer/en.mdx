Given the function <InlineMath math="y = \sqrt{x^2 - 7}" />, then its derivative is

<BlockMath math="y' = \frac{x}{\sqrt{x^2 - 7}}" />

**Statement (1) True**

The ordinate of the point on the curve with abscissa <InlineMath math="4" /> is <InlineMath math="f(4) = \sqrt{4^2 - 7} = 3" />. The gradient of the tangent line at point <InlineMath math="(4, 3)" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="f'(4) = \frac{4}{\sqrt{4^2 - 7}} = \frac{4}{3}" />
</MathContainer>

The equation of the tangent line is found

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="y - 3 = \frac{4}{3}(x - 4)" />

<BlockMath math="y - 3 = \frac{4}{3}x - \frac{16}{3}" />

<BlockMath math="y = \frac{4}{3}x - \frac{16}{3} + 3" />

<BlockMath math="y = \frac{4}{3}x - \frac{7}{3}" />
</MathContainer>

**Statement (2) False**

For values of <InlineMath math="x" /> and <InlineMath math="y" /> that satisfy the condition, by squaring both sides, then

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="y = \sqrt{x^2 - 7}" />

<BlockMath math="y^2 = (\sqrt{x^2 - 7})^2" />

<BlockMath math="y^2 = x^2 - 7" />

<BlockMath math="x^2 - y^2 = 7" />
</MathContainer>

The form <InlineMath math="x^2 - y^2 = 7" /> is not the equation of a circle.

**Statement (3) True**

The line <InlineMath math="y = -\frac{3}{4}x + 6" /> has gradient <InlineMath math="m_1 = -\frac{3}{4}" />. From Statement (1), the gradient of the tangent line at <InlineMath math="x = 4" /> is <InlineMath math="m_2 = \frac{4}{3}" />. Because <InlineMath math="m_1m_2 = -1" />, both are perpendicular.

**Statement (4) False**

The curve does not pass through the point <InlineMath math="(4, -3)" />, because the curve passes through the point <InlineMath math="(4, 3)" />.

So, the correct statements are (1) and (3).
