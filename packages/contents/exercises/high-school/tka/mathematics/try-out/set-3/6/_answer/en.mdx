Given <InlineMath math="u_3 = a" />, <InlineMath math="u_4 = \frac{1}{a}" /> and <InlineMath math="u_5 = \frac{1}{a^2 + 2a}" /> with <InlineMath math="a \neq 0" /> and ratio <InlineMath math="r \neq 1" />. Then the value of one term with another consecutive term must not be the same.

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="\frac{u_4}{u_3} = \frac{u_5}{u_4}" />

<BlockMath math="u_4 \cdot u_4 = u_3 \cdot u_5" />

<BlockMath math="\frac{1}{a} \cdot \frac{1}{a} = a \cdot \frac{1}{a^2 + 2a}" />

<BlockMath math="\frac{1}{a^2} = \frac{a}{a(a + 2)}" />

<BlockMath math="\frac{1}{a^2} = \frac{1}{a + 2}" />

<BlockMath math="a^2 = a + 2" />

<BlockMath math="a^2 - a - 2 = 0" />
</MathContainer>

Factorize

<BlockMath math="(a + 1)(a - 2) = 0" />

<BlockMath math="a = -1 \cup a = 2" />

For <InlineMath math="a = -1" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="u_3 = a = -1" />

<BlockMath math="u_4 = \frac{1}{a} = \frac{1}{-1} = -1" />
</MathContainer>

Because <InlineMath math="u_3 = u_4" /> then its ratio is <InlineMath math="1" />. Whereas the condition is <InlineMath math="r \neq 1" />. So <InlineMath math="a = -1" /> does not satisfy.

For <InlineMath math="a = 2" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="u_3 = kr^2 = 2 \quad ...(1)" />

<BlockMath math="u_4 = kr^3 = \frac{1}{2} \quad ...(2)" />
</MathContainer>

Determining the product of the first five terms

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="u_1 \times u_2 \times u_3 \times u_4 \times u_5" />

<BlockMath math="= k \times kr \times kr^2 \times kr^3 \times kr^4" />

<BlockMath math="= k^5 \times r^{10}" />

<BlockMath math="= (kr^2)^5" />

<BlockMath math="= (u_3)^5" />

<BlockMath math="= 2^5" />

<BlockMath math="= 32" />
</MathContainer>
