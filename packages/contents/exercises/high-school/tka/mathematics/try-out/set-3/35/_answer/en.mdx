Understand the concept of the remainder theorem below

<BlockMath math="f(x) : (x - a) \Rightarrow \text{remainder} = f(a)" />

Substitute <InlineMath math="x = a" /> into <InlineMath math="f(x)" /> with the result being the remainder.

Determining the value of <InlineMath math="b" /> in <InlineMath math="g(x)" />

<InlineMath math="g(x) : (x - 1) \Rightarrow \text{remainder} = g(1)" />. Because it is perfectly divisible, the remainder is equal to zero

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="\text{remainder} = g(1)" />

<BlockMath math="0 = g(1)" />

<BlockMath math="0 = 1^3 + 1^2 - 1 + b" />

<BlockMath math="0 = 1 + 1 - 1 + b" />

<BlockMath math="0 = 1 + b" />

<BlockMath math="b = -1" />
</MathContainer>

So the function becomes <InlineMath math="g(x) = x^3 + x^2 - x - 1" />.

Determine the remainder of <InlineMath math="g(x)" /> divided by <InlineMath math="x^2 - 1" /> using long division

<BlockMath math="\begin{array}{c|l}
& x + 1 \\
\hline
x^2 - 1 & x^3 + x^2 - x - 1 \\
& \underline{x^3 + 0x^2 - x \phantom{+ x^2}} \; - \\
& \phantom{x^3 + 0} x^2 - 1 \\
& \phantom{x^3 + 0} \underline{x^2 + 0x - 1} \; - \\
& \phantom{x^3 + x^2 +} 0
\end{array}" />

So the remainder of the division is <InlineMath math="0" />.
