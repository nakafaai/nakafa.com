To solve this integral, use the substitution method by letting <InlineMath math="u = 3x^2 - 2x + 7" />

#### Determining the Substitution

Let <InlineMath math="u = 3x^2 - 2x + 7" />, then its derivative is

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="\frac{du}{dx} = 6x - 2" />

<BlockMath math="du = 2(3x - 1)dx" />
</MathContainer>

From this, we obtain <InlineMath math="(3x - 1)dx = \frac{1}{2}du" />

#### Transforming the Integral

Substitute into the integral

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="\int \frac{3x - 1}{(3x^2 - 2x + 7)^7} dx = \int \frac{1}{u^7} \cdot \frac{1}{2} du" />

<BlockMath math="= \frac{1}{2} \int u^{-7} du" />
</MathContainer>

#### Integrating

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="\frac{1}{2} \int u^{-7} du = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{u^{-6}}{-6} + C" />

<BlockMath math="= \frac{1}{2} \cdot \left(-\frac{1}{6}\right) u^{-6} + C" />

<BlockMath math="= -\frac{1}{12} u^{-6} + C" />
</MathContainer>

#### Substituting Back

Substitute back <InlineMath math="u = 3x^2 - 2x + 7" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="-\frac{1}{12} u^{-6} + C = \frac{-1}{12(3x^2 - 2x + 7)^6} + C" />
</MathContainer>

Therefore, the result of <InlineMath math="\int \frac{3x - 1}{(3x^2 - 2x + 7)^7} dx = \frac{-1}{12(3x^2 - 2x + 7)^6} + C" />
