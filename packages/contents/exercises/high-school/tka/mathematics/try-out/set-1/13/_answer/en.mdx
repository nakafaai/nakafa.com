Given matrices <InlineMath math="A = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & y \\ 5 & -1 \end{pmatrix}" />, <InlineMath math="B = \begin{pmatrix} x & 5 \\ -3 & 6 \end{pmatrix}" />, and <InlineMath math="C = \begin{pmatrix} -3 & -1 \\ y & 9 \end{pmatrix}" />

#### Matrix Addition

Calculate <InlineMath math="A + B + C" />

<BlockMath math="\begin{pmatrix} 3 & y \\ 5 & -1 \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} x & 5 \\ -3 & 6 \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} -3 & -1 \\ y & 9 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 8 & 5x \\ -x & -4 \end{pmatrix}" />

Add corresponding elements

<BlockMath math="\begin{pmatrix} 3 + x + (-3) & y + 5 + (-1) \\ 5 + (-3) + y & -1 + 6 + 9 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 8 & 5x \\ -x & -4 \end{pmatrix}" />

Simplify

<BlockMath math="\begin{pmatrix} x & y + 4 \\ 2 + y & 14 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 8 & 5x \\ -x & -4 \end{pmatrix}" />

#### Finding Values of x and y

From element in first row, first column

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="x = 8" />

<BlockMath math="3 + x - (-3) = 8" />

<BlockMath math="3 + x + 3 = 8" />

<BlockMath math="x = 2" />
</MathContainer>

From element in first row, second column

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="y + 4 = 5x" />

<BlockMath math="y + 5 - (-1) = 5x" />

<BlockMath math="y + 5 + 1 = 5x" />

<BlockMath math="y + 6 = 5(2)" />

<BlockMath math="y = 10 - 6 = 4" />
</MathContainer>

#### Calculating Final Result

Substitute values <InlineMath math="x = 2" /> and <InlineMath math="y = 4" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="x + 2xy + y = 2 + 2(2)(4) + 4" />

<BlockMath math="= 2 + 16 + 4 = 22" />
</MathContainer>

Therefore, the value of <InlineMath math="x + 2xy + y = 22" />
