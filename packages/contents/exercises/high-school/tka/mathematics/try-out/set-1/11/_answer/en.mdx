#### Scale Transformation Matrix

Scale matrix <InlineMath math="(T_1)" /> with factor <InlineMath math="2" /> on the x-axis and <InlineMath math="0.5" /> on the y-axis

<BlockMath math="M_1 = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0.5 \end{pmatrix}" />

#### Shear Transformation Matrix

Shear matrix along the x-axis with factor <InlineMath math="1" /> <InlineMath math="(T_2)" />

<BlockMath math="M_2 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}" />

#### Composition Matrix

The composition matrix <InlineMath math="(T_2 \circ T_1)" /> is <InlineMath math="M_2 \times M_1" />

<BlockMath math="M_2 \times M_1 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0.5 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 0.5 \\ 0 & 0.5 \end{pmatrix}" />

#### Calculating Initial Area

A square with side <InlineMath math="2" /> units has an initial area of

<BlockMath math="\text{Initial area} = 2 \times 2 = 4 \text{ square units}" />

#### Determining Area Change Scale Factor

The determinant of the composition matrix <InlineMath math="M_2 \cdot M_1" /> gives the area change scale factor

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="\det(M_2 M_1) = (2 \times 0.5) - (0.5 \times 0)" />

<BlockMath math="= 1 - 0 = 1" />
</MathContainer>

#### Area After Transformation

The area after transformation is calculated by

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="\text{New area} = |\det(M_2 M_1)| \times \text{Initial area}" />

<BlockMath math="= |1| \times 4 = 4 \text{ square units}" />
</MathContainer>

Although scale and shear transformations occurred, the area of the square remains the same because the determinant of the composition matrix is <InlineMath math="1" />

Therefore, the area of the square after both transformations is <InlineMath math="4" /> square units
