Let capsule <InlineMath math="= x" />, tablet <InlineMath math="= y" />, with <InlineMath math="x \geq 0" /> and <InlineMath math="y \geq 0" />

We obtain the mathematical model

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="5x + 2y \leq 60 \quad \text{...(i)}" />

<BlockMath math="2x + 2y \leq 30 \quad \text{...(ii)}" />
</MathContainer>

#### Finding the Intersection Point

If both line equations are eliminated, we get the intersection point

<BlockMath math="\begin{array}{rcrcrcl} 5x & + & 2y & = & 60 & & \\ 2x & + & 2y & = & 30 & & - \\ \hline 3x & & & = & 30 & & \\ x & & & = & 10 & \Rightarrow & y = 5 \end{array}" />

#### Determining Minimum Cost

The minimum cost function for capsules and tablets is <InlineMath math="f(x, y) = 1{,}000x + 800y" />

Values at each corner point

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="f(12, 0) = 1{,}000(12) + 800(0) = 12{,}000" />

<BlockMath math="f(10, 5) = 1{,}000(10) + 800(5) = 14{,}000" />

<BlockMath math="f(0, 15) = 1{,}000(0) + 800(15) = 12{,}000" />
</MathContainer>

Therefore, the minimum cost that must be spent to meet the calcium and iron needs of the toddler is <InlineMath math="\text{Rp}12{,}000" />
