Given curve <InlineMath math="y = 6\sqrt{x}" /> with tangent point <InlineMath math="x = 9" />

#### Coordinates of Tangent Point

For <InlineMath math="x = 9" />, then

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="y = 6\sqrt{9}" />

<BlockMath math="= 6 \cdot 3 = 18" />
</MathContainer>

The coordinates of the tangent point are <InlineMath math="(9, 18)" />

#### Gradient of Tangent Line

Calculate the derivative of the function <InlineMath math="f'(x) = m" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="f(x) = 6\sqrt{x} = 6x^{\frac{1}{2}}" />

<BlockMath math="f'(x) = 6 \cdot \frac{1}{2}x^{-\frac{1}{2}}" />

<BlockMath math="= \frac{3}{x^{\frac{1}{2}}} = \frac{3}{\sqrt{x}}" />
</MathContainer>

Gradient at <InlineMath math="x = 9" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="f'(9) = \frac{3}{\sqrt{9}} = \frac{3}{3} = 1" />
</MathContainer>

#### Tangent Line Equation

Tangent line equation at point <InlineMath math="(9, 18)" /> with <InlineMath math="m = 1" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)" />

<BlockMath math="y - 18 = 1(x - 9)" />

<BlockMath math="y = x - 9 + 18" />

<BlockMath math="y = x + 9" />
</MathContainer>

Therefore, the equation of the tangent line to the curve is <InlineMath math="y = x + 9" />
