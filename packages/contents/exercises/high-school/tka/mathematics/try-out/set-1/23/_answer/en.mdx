#### Consider Triangle TQR

Calculate the length of <InlineMath math="TR" /> using the Pythagorean theorem on the right triangle at the base

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="TR = \sqrt{QR^2 + QT^2}" />

<BlockMath math="= \sqrt{3^2 + 3^2}" />

<BlockMath math="= \sqrt{18} = 3\sqrt{2}" />
</MathContainer>

#### Calculating TO

Since <InlineMath math="O" /> is the center of the base, <InlineMath math="TO" /> is half of the diagonal <InlineMath math="TR" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="TO = \frac{1}{2}TR" />

<BlockMath math="= \frac{1}{2} \cdot 3\sqrt{2}" />

<BlockMath math="= \frac{3}{2}\sqrt{2}" />
</MathContainer>

#### Calculating PO

Use the Pythagorean theorem on triangle <InlineMath math="PTO" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="PO = \sqrt{PR^2 - RO^2}" />

<BlockMath math="= \sqrt{(3\sqrt{2})^2 - \left(\frac{3}{2}\sqrt{2}\right)^2}" />

<BlockMath math="= \sqrt{18 - \frac{9}{2}}" />

<BlockMath math="= \sqrt{\frac{27}{2}}" />
</MathContainer>

#### Calculating Tangent of Angle

The tangent of angle <InlineMath math="\alpha" /> between line PT and base QRST

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="\tan \alpha = \frac{PO}{TO}" />

<BlockMath math="= \frac{\sqrt{\frac{27}{2}}}{\frac{3}{2}\sqrt{2}}" />

<BlockMath math="= \sqrt{\frac{27}{2}} \cdot \frac{2}{3\sqrt{2}}" />

<BlockMath math="= \frac{2\sqrt{27}}{3\sqrt{2} \cdot \sqrt{2}}" />

<BlockMath math="= \frac{2 \cdot 3\sqrt{3}}{3 \cdot 2} = \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{3} = \sqrt{3}" />
</MathContainer>

Therefore, the tangent of the angle between line PT and base QRST is <InlineMath math="\sqrt{3}" />
