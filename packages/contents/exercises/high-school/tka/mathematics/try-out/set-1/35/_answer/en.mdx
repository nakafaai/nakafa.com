To calculate the area of the region bounded by both curves, first find the intersection points between the parabola and the line.

#### Finding Intersection Points

The intersection points are obtained by equating both equations

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="x^2 - 4x + 3 = 3 - x" />

<BlockMath math="x^2 - 3x = 0" />

<BlockMath math="x(x - 3) = 0" />
</MathContainer>

Therefore, <InlineMath math="x_1 = 0" /> or <InlineMath math="x_2 = 3" />

#### Determining Upper and Lower Curves

In the interval between <InlineMath math="x = 0" /> and <InlineMath math="x = 3" />, the line <InlineMath math="y = 3 - x" /> is above the parabola <InlineMath math="y = x^2 - 4x + 3" />

#### Calculating the Area

The area of the region is calculated using integration

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="L = \int_{x_1}^{x_2} (y_{\text{upper}} - y_{\text{lower}}) dx" />

<BlockMath math="= \int_0^3 [(3 - x) - (x^2 - 4x + 3)] dx" />

<BlockMath math="= \int_0^3 (-x^2 + 3x) dx" />

<BlockMath math="= \left[-\frac{1}{3}x^3 + \frac{3}{2}x^2\right]_0^3" />

<BlockMath math="= \left(-\frac{1}{3}(3)^3 + \frac{3}{2}(3)^2\right) - \left(-\frac{1}{3}(0)^3 + \frac{3}{2}(0)^2\right)" />

<BlockMath math="= -9 + \frac{27}{2}" />

<BlockMath math="= \frac{9}{2}" />
</MathContainer>

Therefore, the area of the region between the two curves is <InlineMath math="\frac{9}{2}" /> square units.
