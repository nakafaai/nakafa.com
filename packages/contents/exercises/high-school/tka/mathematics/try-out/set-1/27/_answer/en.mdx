To solve the limit <InlineMath math="\lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{1 - \cos 2x}{x \tan 2x}\right)" />, use trigonometric identities

#### Using Trigonometric Identities

Use the identity <InlineMath math="1 - \cos 2x = 2\sin^2 x" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="\lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{1 - \cos 2x}{x \tan 2x}\right) = \lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{2\sin^2 x}{x \tan 2x}\right)" />

<BlockMath math="= \lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{2\sin^2 x}{x \tan 2x}\right)" />
</MathContainer>

#### Separating the Limit

Separate into several parts using limit properties

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="= 2 \lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{\sin x}{x}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{\sin x}{x}\right) \cdot \frac{1}{2x} \cdot \left(\frac{2x}{\tan 2x}\right)" />

<BlockMath math="= 2 \lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{\sin x}{x}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{\sin x}{x}\right) \cdot \frac{1}{2x} \cdot \left(\frac{2x}{\tan 2x}\right)" />
</MathContainer>

Use the fact that <InlineMath math="\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="= 2 \cdot 1 \cdot 1 \cdot \frac{1}{2x} \cdot \lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{2x}{\tan 2x}\right)" />

<BlockMath math="= 2 \cdot \frac{1}{2x} \cdot x = 1" />
</MathContainer>

Therefore, the value of <InlineMath math="\lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{1 - \cos 2x}{x \tan 2x}\right) = 1" />
