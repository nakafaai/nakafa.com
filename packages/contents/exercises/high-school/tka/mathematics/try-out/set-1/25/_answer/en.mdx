Given circle <InlineMath math="L = x^2 + y^2 + 2x - 6y + 2 = 0" /> parallel to line <InlineMath math="x - y + 3 = 0" />

#### Determining the Circle's Radius

The general form of a circle <InlineMath math="(x - a)^2 + (y - b)^2 = r^2" /> can be found from equation <InlineMath math="L" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="x^2 + y^2 + 2x - 6y + 2 = 0" />

<BlockMath math="(x + 1)^2 - 1 + (y - 3)^2 - 9 = -2" />

<BlockMath math="(x + 1)^2 + (y - 3)^2 = -2 + 10" />

<BlockMath math="(x + 1)^2 + (y - 3)^2 = 8" />
</MathContainer>

Therefore, the circle's center is at <InlineMath math="(-1, 3)" /> and radius <InlineMath math="r = \sqrt{8} = 2\sqrt{2}" />

#### Determining the Gradient

Since <InlineMath math="y = mx + c" /> and the line is parallel to the tangent line, the gradient is

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="x - y + 3 = 0 \Leftrightarrow y = x + 3" />

<BlockMath math="m_g = m = 1" />
</MathContainer>

#### Tangent Line Equation

The formula for the tangent line equation to a circle with gradient <InlineMath math="m" />

<BlockMath math="y - b = m(x - a) \pm r\sqrt{1 + m^2}" />

Substitute the known values

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="y - 3 = 1(x + 1) \pm \sqrt{8} \cdot \sqrt{1 + (1)^2}" />

<BlockMath math="y - 3 = x + 1 \pm \sqrt{8} \cdot \sqrt{2}" />

<BlockMath math="y - 3 = x + 1 \pm 4" />
</MathContainer>

We obtain two equations

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="y = x + 4 + 4 \quad \text{or} \quad y = x + 4 - 4" />

<BlockMath math="y = x + 8 \quad \text{or} \quad y = x" />
</MathContainer>

Convert to general form

<BlockMath math="x - y + 8 = 0 \quad \text{or} \quad x - y = 0" />

The most appropriate answer is C: <InlineMath math="x - y + 8 = 0" />
