Given <InlineMath math="A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix}" /> and <InlineMath math="B = \begin{pmatrix} 4 & 1 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix}" /> with <InlineMath math="AC = B" />

#### Finding Matrix C

From the equation <InlineMath math="AC = B" />, multiply both sides by <InlineMath math="A^{-1}" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="AC = B" />

<BlockMath math="A^{-1}AC = A^{-1}B" />

<BlockMath math="C = A^{-1}B" />
</MathContainer>

However, since the order of matrix multiplication matters

<BlockMath math="C = BA^{-1}" />

#### Determining Inverse of Matrix A

For matrix <InlineMath math="A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix}" />, its inverse is

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="A^{-1} = \frac{1}{(1 \cdot 3) - (1 \cdot 2)} \begin{pmatrix} 3 & -2 \\ -1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}" />

<BlockMath math="= \frac{1}{3 - 2} \begin{pmatrix} 3 & -2 \\ -1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}" />

<BlockMath math="= \begin{pmatrix} 3 & -2 \\ -1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}" />
</MathContainer>

#### Calculating Matrix C

Calculate <InlineMath math="C = BA^{-1}" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="C = \begin{pmatrix} 4 & 1 \\ 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 3 & -2 \\ -1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}" />

<BlockMath math="= \begin{pmatrix} 4(3) + 1(-1) & 4(-2) + 1(1) \\ 1(3) + 3(-1) & 1(-2) + 3(1) \end{pmatrix}" />

<BlockMath math="= \begin{pmatrix} 12 - 1 & -8 + 1 \\ 3 - 3 & -2 + 3 \end{pmatrix}" />

<BlockMath math="= \begin{pmatrix} 11 & -7 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}" />
</MathContainer>

#### Calculating Determinant of C

The determinant of matrix <InlineMath math="C" /> is

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="|C| = (11 \cdot 1) - (-7 \cdot 0)" />

<BlockMath math="= 11 - 0 = 11" />
</MathContainer>

Therefore, the determinant of matrix <InlineMath math="C" /> is <InlineMath math="11" />
