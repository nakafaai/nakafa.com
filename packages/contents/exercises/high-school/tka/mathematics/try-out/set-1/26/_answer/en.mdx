To solve the limit <InlineMath math="\lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{5x}{3 - \sqrt{9 + x}}\right)" />, we rationalize the denominator

#### Rationalizing the Denominator

Multiply both numerator and denominator by the conjugate of the denominator, which is <InlineMath math="3 + \sqrt{9 + x}" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="\lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{5x}{3 - \sqrt{9 + x}}\right) = \lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{5x}{3 - \sqrt{9 + x}} \cdot \frac{3 + \sqrt{9 + x}}{3 + \sqrt{9 + x}}\right)" />

<BlockMath math="= \lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{5x(3 + \sqrt{9 + x})}{(3 - \sqrt{9 + x})(3 + \sqrt{9 + x})}\right)" />
</MathContainer>

#### Simplifying the Denominator

Use the identity <InlineMath math="(a - b)(a + b) = a^2 - b^2" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="= \lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{5x(3 + \sqrt{9 + x})}{9 - (9 + x)}\right)" />

<BlockMath math="= \lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{5x(3 + \sqrt{9 + x})}{9 - 9 - x}\right)" />

<BlockMath math="= \lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{5x(3 + \sqrt{9 + x})}{-x}\right)" />
</MathContainer>

#### Simplifying

Simplify by dividing <InlineMath math="x" /> in the numerator and denominator

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="= \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{x(15 + 5\sqrt{9 + x})}{-x}" />

<BlockMath math="= \lim_{x \to 0} (-15 - 5\sqrt{9 + x})" />
</MathContainer>

Substitute <InlineMath math="x = 0" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="= -15 - 5\sqrt{9 + 0}" />

<BlockMath math="= -15 - 5 \cdot 3" />

<BlockMath math="= -15 - 15 = -30" />
</MathContainer>

Therefore, the value of <InlineMath math="\lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{5x}{3 - \sqrt{9 + x}}\right) = -30" />
