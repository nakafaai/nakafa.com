#### Determining Variables

Let the length of the land area <InlineMath math="= x" /> and the width of the land area <InlineMath math="= y" />

#### Wire Constraint

Notice the fence shape consists of <InlineMath math="4" /> layers. This means

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="800 \text{ m} : 4 \text{ layers} = 200 \text{ m/layer}" />
</MathContainer>

Therefore, the fenced land can be expressed as

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="x + 2y = 200" />

<BlockMath math="x = 200 - 2y" />
</MathContainer>

#### Area Function

If the land area <InlineMath math="= xy" />, then

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="L = (200 - 2y) \cdot y" />

<BlockMath math="= 200y - 2y^2" />
</MathContainer>

#### Maximum Area

To find the maximum area, differentiate <InlineMath math="L" /> with respect to <InlineMath math="y" /> and set it equal to <InlineMath math="0" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="L' = 0" />

<BlockMath math="200 - 4y = 0" />

<BlockMath math="4y = 200" />

<BlockMath math="y = 50" />
</MathContainer>

Substitute <InlineMath math="y = 50" /> into the area function

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="L = 200y - 2y^2" />

<BlockMath math="= 200(50) - 2(50)^2" />

<BlockMath math="= 10{,}000 - 5{,}000" />

<BlockMath math="= 5{,}000" />
</MathContainer>

Therefore, the maximum area of the fenced land (in <InlineMath math="\text{m}^2" />) is <InlineMath math="5{,}000 \text{ m}^2" />
