A geometric sequence has a ratio between its terms, or the value of the ratio between terms

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="\frac{b}{a} = \frac{b^2}{b}" />

<BlockMath math="\frac{b}{a} = \frac{b}{1}" />

<BlockMath math="a = 1" />
</MathContainer>

The quadratic equation <InlineMath math="2x^2 + kx + 6 = 0" /> has roots <InlineMath math="x_1 = a = 1" /> and <InlineMath math="x_2 = b" />

Product of roots

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="x_1 \cdot x_2 = \frac{6}{2}" />

<BlockMath math="(1)x_2 = 3" />

<BlockMath math="b = 3" />
</MathContainer>

Sum of roots

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="x_1 + x_2 = \frac{-k}{2}" />

<BlockMath math="1 + 3 = \frac{-k}{2}" />

<BlockMath math="4 = \frac{-k}{2}" />

<BlockMath math="k = -8" />
</MathContainer>

Therefore the geometric sequence is

<BlockMath math="a, b, b^2 = 1, 3, 9, ..." />

Then the <InlineMath math="4" />th term is

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="u_4 = ar^3" />

<BlockMath math="u_4 = (1)(3)^3" />

<BlockMath math="u_4 = 27" />
</MathContainer>

Thus the values of <InlineMath math="u_4" /> and <InlineMath math="k" /> respectively are <InlineMath math="27" /> and <InlineMath math="-8" />.
