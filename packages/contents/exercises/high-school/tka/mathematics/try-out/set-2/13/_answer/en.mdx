If <InlineMath math="y = f(g(x))" /> then the first derivative is <InlineMath math="y' = g'(x) \cdot f'(g(x))" />. Check the limit value

<BlockMath math="\lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(3 + 2h^2) - f(3 - 3h^2)}{h^2} = \frac{0}{0}" />

Then we use another method, L'Hospital's rule or the first derivative

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="\lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(3 + 2h^2) - f(3 - 3h^2)}{h^2}" />

<BlockMath math="= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{4h f'(3 + 2h^2) - (-6h) f'(3 - 3h^2)}{2h}" />

<BlockMath math="= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{4f'(3 + 2h^2) + 6f'(3 - 3h^2)}{2}" />

<BlockMath math="= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{10f'(3 + 2h^2)}{2}" />

<BlockMath math="= \lim_{h \to 0} 5f'(3 + 2h^2)" />

<BlockMath math="= 5f'(3 + 2 \cdot 0^2)" />

<BlockMath math="= 5f'(3)" />

<BlockMath math="= 5 \left(\frac{1}{2\sqrt{1 + 3}}\right)" />

<BlockMath math="= \frac{5}{4}" />
</MathContainer>
