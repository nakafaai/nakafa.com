The mistake in sentence <InlineMath math="(2)" /> lies in the use of a colon (`:`) after the phrase *such as*.

According to spelling guidelines, a colon is used at the end of a complete statement followed by an enumeration. However, a colon must **not** be used if the enumeration is preceded by detailing words like *such as*, *for example*, or *namely*.

To fix it, we can remove the phrase *such as* and directly use a colon (`:`) after the complete statement (after the word *negatively*). Additionally, because this sentence has an enumeration of more than two elements, a comma must be used before the conjunction *and* at the last item.

Therefore, the most appropriate correction is replacing *, such as:* with a colon (`:`) and adding a comma before the word *and*. The corrected sentence segment becomes: *"...grew negatively: Singapore -2.2, Hong Kong -8.9, the European Union -2.7, and China experienced a decline of up to minus 6.8."*
