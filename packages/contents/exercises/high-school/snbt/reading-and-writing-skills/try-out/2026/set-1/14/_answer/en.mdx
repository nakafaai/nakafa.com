The ineffective sentence in the text is sentence <InlineMath math="(4)" />.

An effective sentence must have at least a clear subject and predicate. Consider the following excerpt from sentence <InlineMath math="(4)" />: "**In** natural phenomena, such as volcanoes, storms, earthquakes, and others, they also cause changes to water quality..."

The use of the preposition _In_ at the beginning of the sentence causes the sentence to lose its true subject, as the phrase turns into an adverbial modifier.

To make it an effective sentence with a clear subject, the word _In_ at the beginning of the sentence must be removed so that the sentence becomes: "Natural phenomena, such as volcanoes, storms, earthquakes, and others, also cause changes to water quality..."
