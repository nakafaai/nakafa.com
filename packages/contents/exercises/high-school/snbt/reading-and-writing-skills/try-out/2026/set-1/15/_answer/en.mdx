The core sentence is the main idea or basic structure that builds a sentence, which consists only of the core subject and core predicate (along with the core object if the sentence is active transitive). All additional modifiers, complements, and expanding subordinate clauses must be removed.

Let's dissect the elements of sentence <InlineMath math="(7)" />: "_Water pollution in Indonesia is mostly caused by human activities that leave behind settlement waste, agricultural waste, and industrial waste, including mining._"

- **Core Subject**: Water pollution (the modifier "in Indonesia" is removed)
- **Core Predicate**: is caused (the modifier "mostly by" is removed)
- **Core Complement**: human activities (the expanding clause "that leave behind..." is removed)

By combining these core elements, the core of sentence <InlineMath math="(7)" /> is **Water pollution is caused by human activities**.
