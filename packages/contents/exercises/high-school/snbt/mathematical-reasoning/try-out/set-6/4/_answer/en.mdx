Given:
- Initial capital (<InlineMath math="M_0" />) = <InlineMath math="\text{Rp}15,000,000.00" />
- Final capital (<InlineMath math="M_t" />) = <InlineMath math="\text{Rp}16,500,000.00" />
- Interest rate (<InlineMath math="i" />) = <InlineMath math="12\%" /> per year

First, we calculate the value increase (interest earned):

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Interest} = M_t - M_0" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Interest} = 16,500,000 - 15,000,000" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Interest} = 1,500,000" />
</MathContainer>

The interest rate is <InlineMath math="12\%" /> per year. Thus, the duration Bima saved (<InlineMath math="t" />) can be calculated as:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="t = \frac{\text{Interest}}{M_0 \times i}" />
  <BlockMath math="t = \frac{1,500,000}{15,000,000 \times \frac{12}{100}}" />
  <BlockMath math="t = \frac{1,500,000}{1,800,000}" />
  <BlockMath math="t = \frac{15}{18} = \frac{5}{6} \text{ year}" />
</MathContainer>

Converting to months:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="t = \frac{5}{6} \times 12 \text{ months}" />
  <BlockMath math="t = 5 \times 2 = 10 \text{ months}" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the duration Bima saved is <InlineMath math="10" /> months.
