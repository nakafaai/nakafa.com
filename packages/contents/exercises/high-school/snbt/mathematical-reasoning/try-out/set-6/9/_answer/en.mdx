#### Constructing the Mathematical Model

The basic formula for the phone tariff (<InlineMath math="y" />) is given as:

<BlockMath math="y = (\text{duration} \times \text{rate per minute}) + \text{subscription fee}" />

From the problem, we have the following information:
1. Call duration = <InlineMath math="x" /> minutes.
2. Subscription fee (<InlineMath math="z" />) = <InlineMath math="60,000" />.
3. The rate per minute is <InlineMath math="150" /> more than the call duration. This means:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Rate per minute} = x + 150" />
</MathContainer>

#### Substitution into the Formula

We substitute these components into the initial formula:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="y = x(x + 150) + 60,000" />
</MathContainer>

Expand the multiplication:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="y = x^2 + 150x + 60,000" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the function for the landline tariff paid by the customer is <InlineMath math="y = x^2 + 150x + 60,000" />.
