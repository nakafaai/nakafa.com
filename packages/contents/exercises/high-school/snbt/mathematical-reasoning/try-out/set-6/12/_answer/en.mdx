#### Identifying Proportions

Given:
- To charge from <InlineMath math="20\%" /> to <InlineMath math="100\%" /> takes <InlineMath math="2.5" /> hours.
- The percentage charged is <InlineMath math="100\% - 20\% = 80\%" />.

Now the battery condition is <InlineMath math="60\%" />.
- The target charge is <InlineMath math="100\%" />.
- The percentage needed to charge is <InlineMath math="100\% - 60\% = 40\%" />.

#### Direct Proportion

Since the charging power is constant, the time required is directly proportional to the percentage charged.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\frac{t_1}{p_1} = \frac{t_2}{p_2}" />
</MathContainer>

Substitute the values:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\frac{2.5}{80\%} = \frac{t_2}{40\%}" />
  <BlockMath math="t_2 = \frac{2.5 \times 40\%}{80\%}" />
  <BlockMath math="t_2 = \frac{2.5}{2}" />
  <BlockMath math="t_2 = 1.25 \text{ hours}" />
</MathContainer>

#### Time Conversion

Convert <InlineMath math="1.25" /> hours into hours and minutes:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="1.25 \text{ hours} = 1 \text{ hour} + 0.25 \text{ hours}" />
  <BlockMath math="= 1 \text{ hour} + (0.25 \times 60) \text{ minutes}" />
  <BlockMath math="= 1 \text{ hour} + 15 \text{ minutes}" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the time required is 1 hour 15 minutes.
