#### Determining the Ratio

It is given that the wood is cut into 6 pieces, so <InlineMath math="n = 6" />. The lengths of the pieces form a geometric sequence.

We assume the first term is the longest (it works the same way if assumed otherwise for the total sum). Let the first term be <InlineMath math="a = 96" /> and the 6th term be the shortest <InlineMath math="U_6 = 3" />.

The formula for the <InlineMath math="n" />-th term of a geometric sequence is:

<BlockMath math="U_n = ar^{n-1}" />

Substitute the known values:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="U_6 = 96 \cdot r^{6-1}" />
  <BlockMath math="3 = 96 \cdot r^5" />
</MathContainer>

Simplify the equation to find the ratio (<InlineMath math="r" />):

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="r^5 = \frac{3}{96}" />
  <BlockMath math="r^5 = \frac{1}{32}" />
  <BlockMath math="r^5 = \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^5" />
  <BlockMath math="r = \frac{1}{2}" />
</MathContainer>

So, the ratio between the wood pieces is <InlineMath math="\frac{1}{2}" />.

#### Calculating Total Wood Length

The total length of the original wood is the sum of all pieces (<InlineMath math="S_6" />). Since <InlineMath math="r < 1" />, we use the formula:

<BlockMath math="S_n = \frac{a(1 - r^n)}{1 - r}" />

Substitute <InlineMath math="a = 96" />, <InlineMath math="r = \frac{1}{2}" />, and <InlineMath math="n = 6" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="S_6 = \frac{96\left(1 - \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^6\right)}{1 - \frac{1}{2}}" />
  <BlockMath math="S_6 = \frac{96\left(1 - \frac{1}{64}\right)}{\frac{1}{2}}" />
</MathContainer>

Calculate the result:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="S_6 = 96 \cdot \left(\frac{63}{64}\right) \cdot 2" />
  <BlockMath math="S_6 = 192 \cdot \frac{63}{64}" />
  <BlockMath math="S_6 = 3 \cdot 63" />
  <BlockMath math="S_6 = 189" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the total length of the original wooden plank is <InlineMath math="189" /> cm.
