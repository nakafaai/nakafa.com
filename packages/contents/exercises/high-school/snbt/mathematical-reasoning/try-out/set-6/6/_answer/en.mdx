#### Volume Rate Analysis

It is given that the pouring rate is the same for both cones. This means that in the same time <InlineMath math="t" />, the volume of sand poured is equal.

<BlockMath math="V_A = V_B" />

The formula for the volume of a cone is:

<BlockMath math="V = \frac{1}{3}\pi R^2 h" />

#### Substituting Radius and Height Relationships

For cone A, given <InlineMath math="R_1 = h_1" />. Its volume is:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="V_A = \frac{1}{3}\pi (h_1)^2 h_1" />
  <BlockMath math="V_A = \frac{1}{3}\pi h_1^3" />
</MathContainer>

For cone B, given <InlineMath math="R_2 = 2h_2" />. Its volume is:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="V_B = \frac{1}{3}\pi (2h_2)^2 h_2" />
  <BlockMath math="V_B = \frac{1}{3}\pi (4h_2^2) h_2" />
  <BlockMath math="V_B = \frac{4}{3}\pi h_2^3" />
</MathContainer>

#### Determining the Height Ratio

Since <InlineMath math="V_A = V_B" />, we have:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\frac{1}{3}\pi h_1^3 = \frac{4}{3}\pi h_2^3" />
</MathContainer>

Simplify by dividing both sides by <InlineMath math="\frac{1}{3}\pi" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="h_1^3 = 4h_2^3" />
</MathContainer>

Arrange into a ratio:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\frac{h_1^3}{h_2^3} = \frac{4}{1}" />
  <BlockMath math="\left(\frac{h_1}{h_2}\right)^3 = 4" />
</MathContainer>

Take the cube root of both sides:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\frac{h_1}{h_2} = \sqrt[3]{4}" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the ratio of heights is <InlineMath math="h_1 : h_2 = \sqrt[3]{4} : 1" />.
