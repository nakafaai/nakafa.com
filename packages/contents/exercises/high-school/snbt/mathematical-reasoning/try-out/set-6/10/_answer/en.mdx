#### Identifying the Concept

This problem involves arranging <InlineMath math="3" /> people from <InlineMath math="7" /> candidates to fill specific positions (chairman, secretary, treasurer). Since the order or position matters (chairman <InlineMath math="\neq" /> secretary <InlineMath math="\neq" /> treasurer), we use the concept of **Permutation**.

#### Calculating Permutation

The formula for the permutation of <InlineMath math="r" /> objects from <InlineMath math="n" /> objects is:

<BlockMath math="P^n_r = \frac{n!}{(n - r)!}" />

Given:
- Number of candidates (<InlineMath math="n" />) = <InlineMath math="7" />
- Number of positions chosen (<InlineMath math="r" />) = <InlineMath math="3" />

Substitute into the formula:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="P^7_3 = \frac{7!}{(7 - 3)!}" />
  <BlockMath math="P^7_3 = \frac{7!}{4!}" />
</MathContainer>

Expand the factorials:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="P^7_3 = \frac{7 \times 6 \times 5 \times 4!}{4!}" />
  <BlockMath math="P^7_3 = 7 \times 6 \times 5" />
  <BlockMath math="P^7_3 = 210" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the number of possible staff committee arrangements is <InlineMath math="210" /> ways.
