#### Defining Variables

Let:
- Salary of Group <InlineMath math="A" /> be <InlineMath math="x" />.
- Salary of Group <InlineMath math="B" /> be <InlineMath math="y" />.
- Salary of Group <InlineMath math="C" /> be <InlineMath math="z" />.

#### Forming Equations

From the problem, we know:
1. Salary of Group <InlineMath math="A" /> is one-third of Group <InlineMath math="B" />'s salary.

   <MathContainer>
     <BlockMath math="x = \frac{1}{3}y \quad \dots (1)" />
   </MathContainer>

2. Salary of Group <InlineMath math="C" /> is half of Group <InlineMath math="B" />'s salary.

   <MathContainer>
     <BlockMath math="z = \frac{1}{2}y \quad \dots (2)" />

#### Solving the Problem

Asked: Income of Group <InlineMath math="A" /> for <InlineMath math="6" /> months is equal to income of Group <InlineMath math="C" /> for <InlineMath math="n" /> months.

The equation is:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="6x = n \cdot z" />
</MathContainer>

Substitute equations <InlineMath math="(1)" /> and <InlineMath math="(2)" /> into the equation above (express everything in terms of <InlineMath math="y" />):

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="6\left(\frac{1}{3}y\right) = n\left(\frac{1}{2}y\right)" />
  <BlockMath math="2y = \frac{n}{2}y" />
</MathContainer>

Since <InlineMath math="y \neq 0" />, we can divide both sides by <InlineMath math="y" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="2 = \frac{n}{2}" />
  <BlockMath math="n = 2 \times 2" />
  <BlockMath math="n = 4" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the income will be equal to the income of Group <InlineMath math="C" /> employees for <InlineMath math="4" /> months.
