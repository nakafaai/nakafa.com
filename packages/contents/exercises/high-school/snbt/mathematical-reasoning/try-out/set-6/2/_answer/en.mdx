It is known that runner <InlineMath math="A" /> takes <InlineMath math="12" /> minutes to complete <InlineMath math="1" /> full lap. First, we convert this time into seconds:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="12 \text{ minutes} = 12 \times 60 = 720 \text{ seconds}" />
</MathContainer>

The average speed of runner <InlineMath math="A" /> is <InlineMath math="2" /> meters per second. We know that speed (<InlineMath math="v" />) is the ratio of distance (<InlineMath math="s" />) per unit of time (<InlineMath math="t" />), as per the formula:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="v = \frac{s}{t}" />
</MathContainer>

The question asks for the length of the track (<InlineMath math="s" />):

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="s = v \times t" />
  <BlockMath math="s = 2 \times 720" />
  <BlockMath math="s = 1440 \text{ meters}" />
</MathContainer>

Converting to kilometers:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="1440 \text{ meters} = \frac{1440}{1000} \text{ Km} = 1.44 \text{ Km}" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the length of the track covered is <InlineMath math="1440" /> meters or <InlineMath math="1.44" /> Km.
