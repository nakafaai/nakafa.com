#### Defining Variables

Let:
- <InlineMath math="\bar{x} = 62" /> be the combined average.
- <InlineMath math="\bar{x}_L = 50" /> be the average score of male students.
- <InlineMath math="\bar{x}_P = 70" /> be the average score of female students.
- <InlineMath math="n_L" /> be the number of male students.
- <InlineMath math="n_P" /> be the number of female students.

#### Using the Combined Average Formula

The formula for the combined average is:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\bar{x} = \frac{n_L \cdot \bar{x}_L + n_P \cdot \bar{x}_P}{n_L + n_P}" />
</MathContainer>

Substitute the known values:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="62 = \frac{50n_L + 70n_P}{n_L + n_P}" />
</MathContainer>

Multiply both sides by <InlineMath math="(n_L + n_P)" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="62(n_L + n_P) = 50n_L + 70n_P" />
  <BlockMath math="62n_L + 62n_P = 50n_L + 70n_P" />
</MathContainer>

Group like terms:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="62n_L - 50n_L = 70n_P - 62n_P" />
  <BlockMath math="12n_L = 8n_P" />
</MathContainer>

#### Determining the Ratio

To find the ratio <InlineMath math="n_L : n_P" />, rearrange the equation:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\frac{n_L}{n_P} = \frac{8}{12}" />
</MathContainer>

Simplify the fraction by dividing both numerator and denominator by <InlineMath math="4" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\frac{n_L}{n_P} = \frac{2}{3}" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the ratio of the number of male students to female students is <InlineMath math="2 : 3" />.
