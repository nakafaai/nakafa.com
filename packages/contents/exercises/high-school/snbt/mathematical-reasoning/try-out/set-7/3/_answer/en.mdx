Here is the calculation of sales for each item (Recall that <InlineMath math="1" /> dozen = <InlineMath math="12" /> pieces/copies).

#### Notebooks

The purchase capital for <InlineMath math="6" /> dozens of notebooks is <InlineMath math="\text{Rp}144,000.00" />.

Sales:

- <InlineMath math="3" /> dozens (<InlineMath math="36" /> pieces) sold at <InlineMath math="\text{Rp}2,500.00" /> each
- <InlineMath math="2" /> dozens (<InlineMath math="24" /> pieces) sold at <InlineMath math="\text{Rp}2,300.00" /> each
- Remaining <InlineMath math="1" /> dozen (<InlineMath math="12" /> pieces) sold at <InlineMath math="\text{Rp}2,100.00" /> each

Total revenue from notebooks:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Revenue} = (36 \times 2,500) + (24 \times 2,300) + (12 \times 2,100)" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Revenue} = 90,000 + 55,200 + 25,200" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Revenue} = 170,400" />
</MathContainer>

Profit from notebooks:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Profit} = \text{Revenue} - \text{Capital}" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Profit} = 170,400 - 144,000" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Profit} = 26,400" />
</MathContainer>

#### Ballpoints
