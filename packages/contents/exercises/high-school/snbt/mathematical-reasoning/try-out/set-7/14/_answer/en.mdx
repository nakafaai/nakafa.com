#### Creating Mathematical Model

Let:
- <InlineMath math="A" /> = capital amount for product <InlineMath math="A" />
- <InlineMath math="B" /> = capital amount for product <InlineMath math="B" />

Given the total capital is <InlineMath math="\text{Rp}10,000,000.00" />:

<BlockMath math="A + B = 10,000,000 \quad ...(1)" />

Given the total profit is <InlineMath math="\text{Rp}904,000.00" /> with product <InlineMath math="A" /> profit of <InlineMath math="8\%" /> and product <InlineMath math="B" /> profit of <InlineMath math="10\%" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="8\% A + 10\% B = 904,000" />
  <BlockMath math="0.08 A + 0.1 B = 904,000" />
</MathContainer>

Multiply both sides by <InlineMath math="10" /> to simplify the calculation:

<BlockMath math="0.8 A + B = 9,040,000 \quad ...(2)" />

#### Solving the System of Equations

Eliminate variable <InlineMath math="B" /> from equations <InlineMath math="(1)" /> and <InlineMath math="(2)" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="A + B = 10,000,000" />
  <BlockMath math="0.8 A + B = 9,040,000" />
  <BlockMath math="\rule{5cm}{0.4pt} \, -" />
  <BlockMath math="0.2 A = 960,000" />
</MathContainer>

Calculate the value of <InlineMath math="A" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="A = \frac{960,000}{0.2}" />
  <BlockMath math="A = 4,800,000" />
</MathContainer>

Therefore, the capital for product <InlineMath math="A" /> is <InlineMath math="\text{Rp}4,800,000.00" />.
