First, we calculate the total capital (buying price) for <InlineMath math="100" /> durians.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total Capital} = 100 \times \text{Rp}8,000.00 = \text{Rp}800,000.00" />
</MathContainer>

Next, we calculate the total revenue from sales. The sales are divided into two parts: the first <InlineMath math="70" /> fruits and the remaining <InlineMath math="30" /> fruits.

**Revenue from the first 70 fruits:**

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="70 \times \text{Rp}10,000.00 = \text{Rp}700,000.00" />
</MathContainer>

**Revenue from the remaining 30 fruits:**

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="30 \times \text{Rp}7,000.00 = \text{Rp}210,000.00" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the total revenue is:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total Revenue} = \text{Rp}700,000.00 + \text{Rp}210,000.00" />
  <BlockMath math="= \text{Rp}910,000.00" />
</MathContainer>

Since the total revenue is greater than the total capital, the dealer makes a profit. The profit amount is the difference between revenue and capital:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Profit} = \text{Total Revenue} - \text{Total Capital}" />
  <BlockMath math="= \text{Rp}910,000.00 - \text{Rp}800,000.00" />
  <BlockMath math="= \text{Rp}110,000.00" />
</MathContainer>

Finally, we calculate the profit percentage relative to the initial capital:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\%\text{ Profit} = \frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{Capital}} \times 100\%" />
  <BlockMath math="= \frac{110,000}{800,000} \times 100\%" />
  <BlockMath math="= \frac{11}{80} \times 100\%" />
  <BlockMath math="= 13.75\%" />
</MathContainer>

So, the dealer makes a profit of <InlineMath math="13.75\%" />.
