We will sort all apple masses from smallest to largest, then divide them into two groups: the 6 heaviest apples for Ferdian and the 6 lightest apples for Belle.

#### Sorting Apple Mass Data

The combined apple mass data (with <InlineMath math="x = 180" />) is:
157, 163, 182, 171, 167, 180, 180, 175, 162, 154, 161, 170.

Sorted from smallest to largest:

<BlockMath math="154, 157, 161, 162, 163, 167, 170, 171, 175, 180, 180, 182" />

#### Apple Distribution

**Belle (6 lightest apples):**
<BlockMath math="154, 157, 161, 162, 163, 167" />

**Ferdian (6 heaviest apples):**
<BlockMath math="170, 171, 175, 180, 180, 182" />

#### Calculating Total Mass Each

**Total Mass for Belle:**

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total Belle} = 154 + 157 + 161 + 162 + 163 + 167" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total Belle} = 964 \text{ grams}" />
</MathContainer>

**Total Mass for Ferdian:**

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total Ferdian} = 170 + 171 + 175 + 180 + 180 + 182" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total Ferdian} = 1,058 \text{ grams}" />
</MathContainer>

#### Calculating Mass Difference

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Difference} = \text{Total Ferdian} - \text{Total Belle}" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Difference} = 1,058 - 964" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Difference} = 94 \text{ grams}" />
</MathContainer>

So, the difference in apple mass carried by Ferdian and Belle is **<InlineMath math="94 \text{ grams}" />**.
