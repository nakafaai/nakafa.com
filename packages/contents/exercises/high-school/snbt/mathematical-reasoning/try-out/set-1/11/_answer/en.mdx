We will calculate the volume of sand in one full truck bed and multiply it by the price per cubic meter.

#### Calculating Truck Bed Volume

The truck bed is shaped like a cuboid with dimensions:
- Length (<InlineMath math="p" />) = <InlineMath math="4 \text{ m}" />
- Width (<InlineMath math="l" />) = <InlineMath math="2 \text{ m}" />
- Height (<InlineMath math="t" />) = <InlineMath math="1.5 \text{ m}" />

The formula for the volume of a cuboid is:

<BlockMath math="V = p \times l \times t" />

We substitute the values:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="V = 4 \times 2 \times 1.5" />
  <BlockMath math="V = 8 \times 1.5" />
  <BlockMath math="V = 12 \text{ m}^3" />
</MathContainer>

#### Calculating Sand Price

The price of sand per cubic meter is <InlineMath math="\text{Rp}300,000.00" />. Thus, the total price for <InlineMath math="12 \text{ m}^3" /> is:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total Price} = \text{Volume} \times \text{Unit Price}" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total Price} = 12 \times 300,000" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total Price} = 3,600,000" />
</MathContainer>

So, the price of sand transported in one full truck bed is <InlineMath math="\text{Rp}3,600,000.00" />.
