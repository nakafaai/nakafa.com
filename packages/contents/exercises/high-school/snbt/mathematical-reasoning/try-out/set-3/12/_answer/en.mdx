**Ratio Analysis**

The ratio of visitors on Monday, Tuesday, and Wednesday is given as <InlineMath math="3 : 4 : 5" />. The total number of visitors for these three days is <InlineMath math="144" />.

We can calculate the total number of parts (units) in the ratio:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total Units} = 3 + 4 + 5 = 12 \text{ Units}" />
</MathContainer>

This means <InlineMath math="12" /> units correspond to <InlineMath math="144" /> people.

**Calculating Monday's Visitors**

Monday has a share of <InlineMath math="3" /> units. We use the fraction method to find the number of visitors.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Monday} = \frac{\text{Monday Units}}{\text{Total Units}} \times \text{Total People}" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Monday} = \frac{3}{12} \times 144" />
</MathContainer>

Simplify the fraction <InlineMath math="\frac{3}{12}" /> to <InlineMath math="\frac{1}{4}" />.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Monday} = \frac{1}{4} \times 144" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Monday} = 36" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the number of visitors on Monday is <InlineMath math="36" />.
