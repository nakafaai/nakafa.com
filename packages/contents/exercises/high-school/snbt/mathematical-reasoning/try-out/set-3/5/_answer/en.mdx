**Formula Analysis**

The lateral surface of a cylinder is a rectangle with length <InlineMath math="2\pi r" /> and height <InlineMath math="t" />. Thus, the formula for the lateral surface area is <InlineMath math="L_S = 2\pi r t" />.

We will compare the volume (<InlineMath math="V = \pi r^2 t" />) to the lateral surface area (<InlineMath math="L_S" />).

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\frac{V}{L_S} = \frac{\pi r^2 t}{2\pi r t}" />
  <BlockMath math="\frac{V}{L_S} = \frac{r}{2}" />
</MathContainer>

**Calculating the Ratio**

In the previous question (Number 4), it was determined that the package height <InlineMath math="t = 9 \text{ cm}" />. Since <InlineMath math="r = \frac{1}{2}t" />, then <InlineMath math="r = \frac{9}{2}" />.

Substitute the value of <InlineMath math="r" /> into the ratio equation:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\frac{V}{L_S} = \frac{\frac{9}{2}}{2}" />
  <BlockMath math="\frac{V}{L_S} = \frac{9}{4}" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the ratio is <InlineMath math="9:4" />.
