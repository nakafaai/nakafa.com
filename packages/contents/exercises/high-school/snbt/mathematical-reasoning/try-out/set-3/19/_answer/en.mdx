**Position Analysis**

Let:
- <InlineMath math="A" />: Top of the hill
- <InlineMath math="B" />: Base of the hill (perpendicular to the ground)
- <InlineMath math="C" />: Initial boat position
- <InlineMath math="D" />: Pier position (final boat position)

Given:
- Boat travel distance <InlineMath math="CD = 2\text{ km}" />.
- Initial line of sight distance <InlineMath math="AC = 12\text{ km}" />.
- Initial elevation angle at <InlineMath math="C" /> is <InlineMath math="37^\circ" />.
- Final elevation angle at <InlineMath math="D" /> is <InlineMath math="53^\circ" />.

We need to find the distance from the base of the hill to the end of the pier, which is the length <InlineMath math="BD" />.

**Distance Calculation**

Consider the right-angled triangle <InlineMath math="ABC" />. We can use the cosine ratio for angle <InlineMath math="C" /> (<InlineMath math="37^\circ" />):

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\cos 37^\circ = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}} = \frac{BC}{AC}" />
</MathContainer>

The length <InlineMath math="BC" /> is the total distance from the base of the hill to the initial boat position, consisting of the distance from the pier to the hill base (<InlineMath math="BD" />) and the boat's travel distance (<InlineMath math="CD" />).

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="BC = BD + CD = BD + 2" />
</MathContainer>

Substitute the known values into the cosine equation:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="0.8 = \frac{BD + 2}{12}" />
  <BlockMath math="0.8 \times 12 = BD + 2" />
  <BlockMath math="9.6 = BD + 2" />
  <BlockMath math="BD = 9.6 - 2" />
  <BlockMath math="BD = 7.6\text{ km}" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the distance from the base of the hill to the end of the pier is <InlineMath math="7.6\text{ km}" />.
