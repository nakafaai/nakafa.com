**Concept of Break-Even Point**

The Break-Even Point is the condition where there is neither profit nor loss, meaning total revenue equals total cost.

  <BlockMath math="B(x) = P(x)" />
</MathContainer>

**Solving the Equation**

Substitute the given functions:

To make calculation easier, multiply both sides by <InlineMath math="10" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="x^2 - 200x + 3600 = 0" />

**Determining the Value of x**

We use quadratic factorization. We look for two numbers that multiply to <InlineMath math="3600" /> and add up to <InlineMath math="-200" />. These numbers are <InlineMath math="-180" /> and <InlineMath math="-20" />.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="(x - 180)(x - 20) = 0" />
</MathContainer>

The roots are:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="x_1 = 180 \quad \text{or} \quad x_2 = 20" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the break-even point occurs when the number of products is <InlineMath math="180" /> units and <InlineMath math="20" /> units.
