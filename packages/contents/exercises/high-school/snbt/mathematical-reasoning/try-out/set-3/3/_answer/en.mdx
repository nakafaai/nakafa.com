**Net Weight Analysis (Netto)**

First, we calculate how much watermelon is actually sold by subtracting the tare (packaging weight) from the gross weight (bruto).

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Tare} = 5\% \times 100 \text{ kg} = 5 \text{ kg}" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Netto} = 100 \text{ kg} - 5 \text{ kg} = 95 \text{ kg}" />
</MathContainer>

**Total Revenue (Selling Price)**

Next, we calculate the total revenue from the watermelon sales. The remaining weight of the watermelons is <InlineMath math="95 - 60 - 20 = 15 \text{ kg}" />.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Sale}_1 = 60 \times 5,000 = \text{Rp}300,000.00" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Sale}_2 = 20 \times 4,000 = \text{Rp}80,000.00" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Sale}_3 = 15 \times 3,000 = \text{Rp}45,000.00" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total Revenue} = 300,000 + 80,000 + 45,000 = \text{Rp}425,000.00" />
</MathContainer>

**Calculating Purchase Price**

The merchant makes a <InlineMath math="70\%" /> profit, meaning the Selling Price is <InlineMath math="170\%" /> of the Purchase Price.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Selling Price} = \text{Purchase Price} + \text{Profit}" />
  <BlockMath math="425,000 = \text{HB} + 0.7 \text{HB}" />
  <BlockMath math="425,000 = 1.7 \text{HB}" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{HB} = \frac{425,000}{1.7} = \text{Rp}250,000.00" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the purchase price for one crate of watermelons is <InlineMath math="\text{Rp}250,000.00" />.
