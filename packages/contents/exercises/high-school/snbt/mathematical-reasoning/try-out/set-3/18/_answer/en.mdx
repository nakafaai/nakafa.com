We will find the height of the helicopter from the helipad (<InlineMath math="DC" />) using trigonometric ratios in right-angled triangles.

Observe the geometry:
- <InlineMath math="AD = 16" /> m (building height).
- <InlineMath math="AB" /> is the distance from the building to the tip of the shadow.
- The angle of elevation to the top of the building is <InlineMath math="30^\circ" /> and to the helicopter is <InlineMath math="45^\circ" />.

First, we calculate the distance <InlineMath math="AB" /> using <InlineMath math="\tan(30^\circ)" /> in triangle <InlineMath math="ABD" />.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\tan(30^\circ) = \frac{AD}{AB}" />
  <BlockMath math="\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{16}{AB}" />
  <BlockMath math="AB = 16\sqrt{3}" />
</MathContainer>

Next, use this distance <InlineMath math="AB" /> to find the total height of the helicopter from the ground (<InlineMath math="AC" />) using <InlineMath math="\tan(45^\circ)" /> in triangle <InlineMath math="ABC" />.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\tan(45^\circ) = \frac{AC}{AB}" />
  <BlockMath math="1 = \frac{AC}{16\sqrt{3}}" />
  <BlockMath math="AC = 16\sqrt{3}" />
</MathContainer>

The height of the helicopter from the helipad is the difference between the total height and the building height.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="DC = AC - AD" />
  <BlockMath math="DC = 16\sqrt{3} - 16" />
  <BlockMath math="DC = 16(\sqrt{3} - 1)" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the height of the helicopter from the helipad is <InlineMath math="16(\sqrt{3} - 1)" /> meters.
