**Work Rate Analysis**

Work rate is the portion of work completed per unit of time (1 minute). Based on the problem:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Rate I} = \frac{1}{16} \text{ part/minute}" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Rate II} = \frac{1}{32} \text{ part/minute}" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Rate III} = \frac{1}{32} \text{ part/minute}" />
</MathContainer>

**Combined Work Calculation**

If the three machines work together for <InlineMath math="6" /> minutes, the total work completed is the sum of their rates multiplied by time:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total} = \left(\frac{1}{16} + \frac{1}{32} + \frac{1}{32}\right) \times 6" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total} = \left(\frac{2}{32} + \frac{1}{32} + \frac{1}{32}\right) \times 6" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total} = \frac{4}{32} \times 6" />
</MathContainer>

Simplify the fraction <InlineMath math="\frac{4}{32}" /> to <InlineMath math="\frac{1}{8}" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total} = \frac{1}{8} \times 6 = \frac{6}{8} = \frac{3}{4}" />
</MathContainer>

In decimal form, <InlineMath math="\frac{3}{4}" /> is equal to <InlineMath math="0.75" />.

Thus, the completed work is <InlineMath math="0.75" /> part.
