We focus on the product **"Minyak mu"** which has an initial price of **Rp<InlineMath math="80.000,00" />** per <InlineMath math="5" /> liters. We need to find the price difference after discount at two supermarkets: **Indoapril** and **Betamidi**.

Here is the comparison of the final price calculations:

| Supermarket | Discount | Discount Amount (<InlineMath math="\text{Discount} \times 80.000" />) | Final Price (<InlineMath math="80.000 - \text{Amount}" />) |
| :--- | :---: | :--- | :--- |
| **Indoapril** | <InlineMath math="25\%" /> | <InlineMath math="25\% \times 80.000 = 20.000" /> | <InlineMath math="80.000 - 20.000 = 60.000" /> |
| **Betamidi** | <InlineMath math="30\%" /> | <InlineMath math="30\% \times 80.000 = 24.000" /> | <InlineMath math="80.000 - 24.000 = 56.000" /> |

From the table above, it is clear that the price of "Minyak mu" at Indoapril is Rp<InlineMath math="60.000,00" /> and at Betamidi is Rp<InlineMath math="56.000,00" />.

The price difference between the two can be easily calculated:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Difference} = \text{Price at Indoapril} - \text{Price at Betamidi}" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Difference} = 60.000 - 56.000 = 4.000" />
</MathContainer>

So, the price difference of "Minyak mu" at these two supermarkets is **Rp<InlineMath math="4.000,00" />**.
