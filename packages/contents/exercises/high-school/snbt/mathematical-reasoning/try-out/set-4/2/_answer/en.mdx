First, let's identify the problem constraints:

1.  Total budget: **Rp<InlineMath math="400.000,00" />**.
2.  Total items: **<InlineMath math="4" /> bouquets**.
3.  Mandatory purchase: At least **<InlineMath math="1" /> Snack Bouquet** (Rp<InlineMath math="70.000,00" />).

The first step is to subtract the price of the mandatory item from the total budget to find the remaining funds.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Remaining Budget} = \text{Total Budget} - \text{Price of Snack}" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Remaining Budget} = 400.000 - 70.000 = 330.000" />
</MathContainer>

Now we have a remaining budget of **Rp<InlineMath math="330.000,00" />** to buy **<InlineMath math="3" /> more bouquets** (since the desired total is <InlineMath math="4" /> items, and <InlineMath math="1" /> is already accounted for).

Let's check possible combinations to spend Rp<InlineMath math="330.000,00" /> on <InlineMath math="3" /> items. Looking at the price list, a **Money Bouquet** costs Rp<InlineMath math="130.000,00" />.

If we buy **<InlineMath math="2" /> Money Bouquets**:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="2 \times 130.000 = 260.000" />
</MathContainer>

The remaining money after buying 2 Money Bouquets is:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="330.000 - 260.000 = 70.000" />
</MathContainer>

This remaining **Rp<InlineMath math="70.000,00" />** is exactly enough to buy **<InlineMath math="1" /> more Snack Bouquet**.

So, the breakdown of our total purchase is:

| Item | Quantity | Unit Price | Total Price |
| :--- | :---: | :--- | :--- |
| **Snack Bouquet (Mandatory)** | <InlineMath math="1" /> | Rp<InlineMath math="70.000" /> | Rp<InlineMath math="70.000" /> |
| **Money Bouquet** | <InlineMath math="2" /> | Rp<InlineMath math="130.000" /> | Rp<InlineMath math="260.000" /> |
| **Snack Bouquet (Additional)** | <InlineMath math="1" /> | Rp<InlineMath math="70.000" /> | Rp<InlineMath math="70.000" /> |
| **Total** | **<InlineMath math="4" />** | | **Rp<InlineMath math="400.000" />** |

The total items purchased are:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="(1 \text{ Mandatory Snack} + 1 \text{ Additional Snack}) + 2 \text{ Money}" />
  <BlockMath math="= 2 \text{ Snack Bouquets} + 2 \text{ Money Bouquets}" />
</MathContainer>

This combination fits both the budget and the item count requirement.
