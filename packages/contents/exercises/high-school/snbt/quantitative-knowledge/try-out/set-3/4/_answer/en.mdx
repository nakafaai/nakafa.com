Given:
*   Initial jacket price = <InlineMath math="\text{Rp}250.000,00" />
*   Discount = <InlineMath math="25\%" />
*   Tax = <InlineMath math="5\%" />

First, we calculate the price after the discount. Since the discount is <InlineMath math="25\%" />, Mrs. Kartika pays <InlineMath math="100\% - 25\% = 75\%" /> of the initial price.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Price after discount} = 75\% \times 250.000" />
  <BlockMath math="= \frac{75}{100} \times 250.000" />
  <BlockMath math="= 187.500" />
</MathContainer>

Next, a tax of <InlineMath math="5\%" /> is applied to the price after discount. Thus, the total to be paid is <InlineMath math="100\% + 5\% = 105\%" /> of the price after discount.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Price to pay} = 105\% \times 187.500" />
  <BlockMath math="= \frac{105}{100} \times 187.500" />
  <BlockMath math="= 1,05 \times 187.500" />
  <BlockMath math="= 196.875" />
</MathContainer>

Directly, the calculation can be written as:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Price} = (100 - 25)\% \times (100 + 5)\% \times 250.000" />
  <BlockMath math="= 75\% \times 105\% \times 250.000" />
  <BlockMath math="= 0,75 \times 1,05 \times 250.000" />
  <BlockMath math="= 196.875" />
</MathContainer>

So, the price Mrs. Kartika has to pay is <InlineMath math="\text{Rp}196.875,00" />.
