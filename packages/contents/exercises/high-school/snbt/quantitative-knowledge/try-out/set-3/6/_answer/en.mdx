Let's analyze each statement for the line <InlineMath math="5x - 10y = 3" />.

**Statement 1: Gradient**

The equation of a line in the form <InlineMath math="ax + by = c" /> has a gradient <InlineMath math="m = -\frac{a}{b}" />.
For <InlineMath math="5x - 10y = 3" />:
*   <InlineMath math="a = 5" />
*   <InlineMath math="b = -10" />

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="m = -\frac{5}{-10} = \frac{1}{2}" />
</MathContainer>

Statement 1 says the gradient is <InlineMath math="-2" />. So, **Statement 1 is false**.

**Statement 2: X-axis intercept**

The x-axis intercept occurs when <InlineMath math="y = 0" />.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="5x - 10(0) = 3" />
  <BlockMath math="5x = 3" />
  <BlockMath math="x = \frac{3}{5}" />
</MathContainer>

The coordinate point is <InlineMath math="\left(\frac{3}{5}, 0\right)" />. So, **Statement 2 is true**.

**Statement 3 and 4: Y-axis intercept**

The y-axis intercept occurs when <InlineMath math="x = 0" />.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="5(0) - 10y = 3" />
  <BlockMath math="-10y = 3" />
  <BlockMath math="y = -\frac{3}{10}" />
</MathContainer>

The coordinate point is <InlineMath math="\left(0, -\frac{3}{10}\right)" />.
So, **Statement 3 is false** and **Statement 4 is true**.

Conclusion: The correct statements are (2) and (4).
