The relationship between speed (<InlineMath math="v" />) and time (<InlineMath math="t" />) is inversely proportional. This means the product of speed and time is constant (fixed distance).

<BlockMath math="v_1 \times t_1 = v_2 \times t_2" />

From the table, we know:
*   First speed <InlineMath math="v_1 = 96 \text{ km/h}" />
*   First time <InlineMath math="t_1 = 1 \text{ hour}" />
*   Second speed <InlineMath math="v_2 = 72 \text{ km/h}" />

We want to find the second time (<InlineMath math="t_2" />).

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="96 \times 1 = 72 \times t_2" />
  <BlockMath math="96 = 72 \times t_2" />
  <BlockMath math="t_2 = \frac{96}{72}" />
  <BlockMath math="t_2 = \frac{4}{3} \text{ hours}" />
</MathContainer>

We convert the fraction of an hour into hours and minutes.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="t_2 = 1 \frac{1}{3} \text{ hours}" />
  <BlockMath math="t_2 = 1 \text{ hour} + \left(\frac{1}{3} \times 60\right) \text{ minutes}" />
  <BlockMath math="t_2 = 1 \text{ hour } 20 \text{ minutes}" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the time required is <InlineMath math="1 \text{ hour } 20 \text{ minutes}" />.
