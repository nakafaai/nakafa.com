We are given matrix <InlineMath math="A" /> as follows:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="A = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -2 \\ x-1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}" />
</MathContainer>

It is known that the determinant of matrix <InlineMath math="A" /> is <InlineMath math="4" />. The determinant formula for a <InlineMath math="2 \times 2" /> matrix like <InlineMath math="\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}" /> is <InlineMath math="ad - bc" />.

#### Calculating the Determinant

We will calculate the determinant of matrix <InlineMath math="A" /> and set it equal to <InlineMath math="4" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\begin{aligned} \det(A) &= (2)(1) - (-2)(x-1) \\ 4 &= 2 - (-2x + 2) \\ 4 &= 2 + 2x - 2 \\ 4 &= 2x \\ x &= \frac{4}{2} \\ x &= 2 \end{aligned}" />
</MathContainer>

So, the value of <InlineMath math="x" /> is <InlineMath math="2" />.
