Let:
- <InlineMath math="n(S)" /> = Total number of students in the class
- <InlineMath math="n(P)" /> = Number of students in scouts
- <InlineMath math="n(F)" /> = Number of students in futsal

Given:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="n(S) = 30" />
  <BlockMath math="n(P) = 12" />
  <BlockMath math="n(F) = 15" />
  <BlockMath math="n(P \cap F) = 7" />
</MathContainer>

The number of students who do not participate in either (<InlineMath math="n(P \cup F)^c" />) can be found using the universal set formula:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="n(S) = n(P) + n(F) - n(P \cap F) + n(P \cup F)^c" />
</MathContainer>

Substitute the known values:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="30 = 12 + 15 - 7 + n(P \cup F)^c" />
  <BlockMath math="30 = 20 + n(P \cup F)^c" />
  <BlockMath math="n(P \cup F)^c = 30 - 20" />
  <BlockMath math="n(P \cup F)^c = 10" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the number of students who do not join scouts and futsal extracurriculars is <InlineMath math="10" />.
