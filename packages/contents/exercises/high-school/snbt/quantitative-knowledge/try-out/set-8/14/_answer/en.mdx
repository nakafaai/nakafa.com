The number of ways to choose red balls is:

<BlockMath math="C(5,2) = \frac{5!}{2!3!} = 10" />

The number of ways to choose white balls is:

<BlockMath math="C(4,1) = \frac{4!}{3!1!} = 4" />

The total number of possible ways to choose the balls is:

<BlockMath math="C(12,3) = \frac{12!}{9!3!} = 220" />

Let <InlineMath math="A =" /> The event of drawing <InlineMath math="2" /> red balls and <InlineMath math="1" /> white ball.

The probability of drawing <InlineMath math="2" /> red balls and <InlineMath math="1" /> white ball is:

<BlockMath math="\begin{aligned} P(A) &= \frac{C(5,2)C(4,1)}{C(12,3)} \\ &= \frac{10 \cdot 4}{220} \\ &= \frac{40}{220} \\ &= \frac{2}{11} \end{aligned}" />
