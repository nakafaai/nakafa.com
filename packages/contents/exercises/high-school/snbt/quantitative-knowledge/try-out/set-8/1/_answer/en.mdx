Let <InlineMath math="x = 0.323323323..." />. Since there are three repeating digits (<InlineMath math="323" />), multiply both sides by <InlineMath math="1000" />.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="1000x = 323.323323..." />
</MathContainer>

Next, subtract the original equation <InlineMath math="x = 0.323323..." /> from this equation:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\begin{aligned} 1000x &= 323.323323... \\ x &= 0.323323... \\ \hline 999x &= 323 \end{aligned}" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, we get:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="x = \frac{323}{999}" />
</MathContainer>

So, the common fraction form of the number is <InlineMath math="\frac{323}{999}" />.
