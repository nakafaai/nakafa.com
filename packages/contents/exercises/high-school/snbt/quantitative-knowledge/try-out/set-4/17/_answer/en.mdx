We are asked to determine the measure of angle <InlineMath math="\angle BOC" /> in equilateral triangle <InlineMath math="ABC" /> with the intersection of lines <InlineMath math="AP" />, <InlineMath math="BQ" />, and <InlineMath math="CR" /> at point <InlineMath math="O" />.

#### Analyzing Initial Information

Triangle <InlineMath math="ABC" /> is an equilateral triangle. This means:

*   <InlineMath math="AB = BC = CA" />
*   <InlineMath math="\angle A = \angle B = \angle C = 60^\circ" />

However, we do not yet know the properties of lines <InlineMath math="AP" />, <InlineMath math="BQ" />, and <InlineMath math="CR" /> for certain from the initial problem, except that they intersect at <InlineMath math="O" />.

#### Analyzing Statement (1)

Statement (1): <InlineMath math="BC = 10 \text{ cm}" />.

This information only provides the length of the triangle's side. Without knowing the properties of lines <InlineMath math="BQ" /> and <InlineMath math="CR" /> (whether they are altitudes, angle bisectors, or medians), we cannot determine the position of point <InlineMath math="O" /> and the angles within it with certainty.

So, statement (1) ALONE is **not sufficient**.

#### Analyzing Statement (2)

Statement (2): Lines <InlineMath math="AP" />, <InlineMath math="BQ" />, and <InlineMath math="CR" /> are angle bisectors of triangle <InlineMath math="ABC" />.

If <InlineMath math="BQ" /> is the angle bisector of angle <InlineMath math="B" />, then:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\angle OB C = \frac{1}{2} \times \angle B = \frac{1}{2} \times 60^\circ = 30^\circ" />
</MathContainer>

If <InlineMath math="CR" /> is the angle bisector of angle <InlineMath math="C" />, then:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\angle OCB = \frac{1}{2} \times \angle C = \frac{1}{2} \times 60^\circ = 30^\circ" />
</MathContainer>

Now consider triangle <InlineMath math="BOC" />. The sum of angles in a triangle is <InlineMath math="180^\circ" />.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\angle BOC + \angle OBC + \angle OCB = 180^\circ" />
  <BlockMath math="\angle BOC + 30^\circ + 30^\circ = 180^\circ" />
  <BlockMath math="\angle BOC + 60^\circ = 180^\circ" />
  <BlockMath math="\angle BOC = 120^\circ" />
</MathContainer>

We can determine the measure of angle <InlineMath math="\angle BOC" /> using only statement (2).

So, statement (2) ALONE is **sufficient**.

#### Conclusion

Statement (1) is not sufficient, but statement (2) is sufficient.
