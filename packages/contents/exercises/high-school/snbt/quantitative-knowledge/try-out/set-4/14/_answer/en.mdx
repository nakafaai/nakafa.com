Given sorted data: <InlineMath math="1, 1, 2, x, 10, y, 17, 18" /> (total <InlineMath math="8" /> data points).

The mean and median of the data are <InlineMath math="9" />.

#### Finding the Value of x from Median

Since there are <InlineMath math="8" /> data points (even), the median is the average of the 4th and 5th data points.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Median} = \frac{x + 10}{2} = 9" />
  <BlockMath math="x + 10 = 18" />
  <BlockMath math="x = 8" />
</MathContainer>

#### Finding the Value of y from Mean

The total of all data divided by the number of data equals the mean.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\bar{x} = \frac{1 + 1 + 2 + 8 + 10 + y + 17 + 18}{8} = 9" />
  <BlockMath math="\frac{57 + y}{8} = 9" />
  <BlockMath math="57 + y = 72" />
  <BlockMath math="y = 15" />
</MathContainer>

#### Calculating the Average of the Last 4 Numbers

Complete data: <InlineMath math="1, 1, 2, 8, 10, 15, 17, 18" />.

The last 4 numbers are: <InlineMath math="10, 15, 17, 18" />.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="P = \frac{10 + 15 + 17 + 18}{4} = \frac{60}{4} = 15" />
</MathContainer>

#### Comparing P and Q

The value <InlineMath math="P = 15" /> and <InlineMath math="Q = 15" />.

Therefore, <InlineMath math="P = Q" />.
