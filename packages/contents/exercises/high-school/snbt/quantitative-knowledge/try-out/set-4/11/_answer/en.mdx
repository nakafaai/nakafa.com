Given the equation <InlineMath math="(f(x))^2 - 6f(x) = -8" /> with <InlineMath math="f(x) = 2x - 4" />.

First, we rewrite the equation into the general form of a quadratic equation:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="(f(x))^2 - 6f(x) + 8 = 0" />
</MathContainer>

Then we substitute <InlineMath math="f(x) = 2x - 4" /> into the equation:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="(2x - 4)^2 - 6(2x - 4) + 8 = 0" />
</MathContainer>

Expand the quadratic form and simplify the equation:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="(4x^2 - 16x + 16) - (12x - 24) + 8 = 0" />
  <BlockMath math="4x^2 - 16x - 12x + 16 + 24 + 8 = 0" />
  <BlockMath math="4x^2 - 28x + 48 = 0" />
</MathContainer>

From the quadratic equation <InlineMath math="ax^2 + bx + c = 0" />, the product of the roots (<InlineMath math="x_1 \cdot x_2" />) is given by the formula <InlineMath math="\frac{c}{a}" />.

Given:
<InlineMath math="a = 4" />
<InlineMath math="b = -28" />
<InlineMath math="c = 48" />

Then:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="x_1 \cdot x_2 = \frac{48}{4} = 12" />
</MathContainer>

So, the value of <InlineMath math="x_1 \cdot x_2" /> is <InlineMath math="12" />.
