Given matrix <InlineMath math="A = \begin{pmatrix} 5 & 3 - x \\ -1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}" /> and its determinant is <InlineMath math="8" />.

The formula for the determinant of a <InlineMath math="2 \times 2" /> matrix <InlineMath math="A = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}" /> is <InlineMath math="\det(A) = ad - bc" />.

Let's calculate the determinant of matrix <InlineMath math="A" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\det(A) = (5)(1) - (3 - x)(-1)" />
  <BlockMath math="\det(A) = 5 - (-(3 - x))" />
  <BlockMath math="\det(A) = 5 + (3 - x)" />
  <BlockMath math="\det(A) = 8 - x" />
</MathContainer>

Given <InlineMath math="\det(A) = 8" />, then:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="8 - x = 8" />
  <BlockMath math="-x = 8 - 8" />
  <BlockMath math="-x = 0" />
  <BlockMath math="x = 0" />
</MathContainer>

So, the value of <InlineMath math="x" /> is <InlineMath math="0" />.
