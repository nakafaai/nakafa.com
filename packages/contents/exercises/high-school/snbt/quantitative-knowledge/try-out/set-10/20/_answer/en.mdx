First, we find the value of <InlineMath math="x" /> from the first equation:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="4x - 16 = 24" />
  <BlockMath math="4x = 24 + 16" />
  <BlockMath math="4x = 40" />
  <BlockMath math="x = \frac{40}{4} = 10" />
</MathContainer>

After finding that <InlineMath math="x = 10" />, we substitute it into the second equation to find the value of <InlineMath math="y" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="2x + y - 16 = 12" />
  <BlockMath math="2(10) + y - 16 = 12" />
  <BlockMath math="20 + y - 16 = 12" />
  <BlockMath math="y + 4 = 12" />
  <BlockMath math="y = 12 - 4" />
  <BlockMath math="y = 8" />
</MathContainer>

Finally, we compare the values of <InlineMath math="x" /> and <InlineMath math="y" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="x = 10" />
  <BlockMath math="y = 8" />
</MathContainer>

Since <InlineMath math="10 > 8" />, then:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="x > y" />
</MathContainer>
