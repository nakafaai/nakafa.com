First, let's calculate the total exam score for Semester <InlineMath math="I" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total Score Semester } I = 5 \times 7.5 = 37.5" />
</MathContainer>

Next, we calculate the total exam score for the whole year (Semester <InlineMath math="I" /> & <InlineMath math="II" />):

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total Score 1 Year} = 11 \times 8 = 88" />
</MathContainer>

To find the total exam score for Semester <InlineMath math="II" />, we subtract the Semester <InlineMath math="I" /> total from the yearly total:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total Score Semester } II = 88 - 37.5 = 50.5" />
</MathContainer>

The number of exams in Semester <InlineMath math="II" /> is the difference between the total exams and Semester <InlineMath math="I" /> exams:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Number of Exams Semester } II = 11 - 5 = 6" />
</MathContainer>

Now we can calculate the average score for Semester <InlineMath math="II" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Average Semester } II = \frac{50.5}{6} \approx 8.4166... \approx 8.42" />
</MathContainer>

Finally, we calculate the increase in the average score from Semester <InlineMath math="I" /> to Semester <InlineMath math="II" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Increase} = \text{Average Semester } II - \text{Average Semester } I" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Increase} = 8.42 - 7.5 = 0.92" />
</MathContainer>

Therefore, the increase in Hani's mathematics exam average is <InlineMath math="0.92" />.
