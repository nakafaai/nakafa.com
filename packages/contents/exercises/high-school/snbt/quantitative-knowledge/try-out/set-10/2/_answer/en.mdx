From the figure, we can see that the angle vertically opposite to <InlineMath math="z^\circ" /> is the angle between the two diagonal lines at the top. The measure of that angle is equal to <InlineMath math="z^\circ" />.

Since the angles on a straight line sum to <InlineMath math="180^\circ" />, the sum of angle <InlineMath math="x^\circ" />, vertical angle <InlineMath math="z^\circ" />, and <InlineMath math="y^\circ" /> is <InlineMath math="180^\circ" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="x^\circ + z^\circ + y^\circ = 180^\circ" />
</MathContainer>

Given that <InlineMath math="x^\circ = y^\circ" />, we can substitute <InlineMath math="y" /> with <InlineMath math="x" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="x^\circ + z^\circ + x^\circ = 180^\circ" />
  <BlockMath math="z^\circ + 2x^\circ = 180^\circ" />
  <BlockMath math="z^\circ = 180^\circ - 2x^\circ" />
</MathContainer>

Therefore, the measure of angle <InlineMath math="z" /> is <InlineMath math="180 - 2x" />.
