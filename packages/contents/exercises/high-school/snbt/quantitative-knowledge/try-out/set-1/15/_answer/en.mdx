Given that triangle <InlineMath math="ABC" /> is similar to triangle <InlineMath math="ADE" />.

From the visualization, we can see the coordinates of the points as follows:
- <InlineMath math="A(2, 1)" />
- <InlineMath math="B(8, 4)" />
- <InlineMath math="C(8, 1)" />
- <InlineMath math="E(2, -3)" />
- <InlineMath math="D(10, 1)" /> (approximate from the diagram)

#### Calculating Side Lengths

For triangle <InlineMath math="ABC" />, which is a right triangle at <InlineMath math="C" />:

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="AC = |8 - 2| = 6" />

<BlockMath math="BC = |4 - 1| = 3" />
</MathContainer>

For triangle <InlineMath math="ADE" />:

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="AE = |1 - (-3)| = 4" />
</MathContainer>

#### Using Similarity

Since both triangles are similar, the ratios of corresponding sides are equal.

<BlockMath math="\frac{AE}{BC} = \frac{AD}{AC}" />

Substitute the known values:

<BlockMath math="\frac{4}{3} = \frac{AD}{6}" />

Multiply both sides by <InlineMath math="6" />:

<BlockMath math="AD = \frac{4 \times 6}{3} = \frac{24}{3} = 8" />

#### Calculating the Area of Triangle ADE

Both triangles have right angles (triangle ABC at C, triangle ADE at A).

The area of a right triangle can be calculated using the formula:

<BlockMath math="\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height}" />

For triangle <InlineMath math="ADE" />:

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="\text{Area of ADE} = \frac{1}{2} \times AD \times AE" />

<BlockMath math="= \frac{1}{2} \times 8 \times 4" />

<BlockMath math="= \frac{32}{2}" />

<BlockMath math="= 16" />
</MathContainer>

Therefore, the area of triangle <InlineMath math="ADE" /> is <InlineMath math="16" />.
