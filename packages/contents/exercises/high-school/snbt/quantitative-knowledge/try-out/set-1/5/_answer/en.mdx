Given that the perimeter of rhombus <InlineMath math="PQRS" /> is <InlineMath math="20" /> units, the length of each side is

<BlockMath math="\text{Side length} = \frac{20}{4} = 5 \text{ units}" />

From the diagram, the coordinates of the rhombus vertices are <InlineMath math="P(0, 0)" />, <InlineMath math="Q(4, 3)" />, <InlineMath math="R(8, 0)" />, and <InlineMath math="S(4, -3)" />.

Verify the length of side <InlineMath math="PQ" />

<BlockMath math="PQ = \sqrt{(4-0)^2 + (3-0)^2} = \sqrt{16 + 9} = \sqrt{25} = 5 \text{ units}" />

This matches the given perimeter.

Calculate the gradient of <InlineMath math="QR" /> using the slope formula

<BlockMath math="m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}" />

With <InlineMath math="Q(4, 3)" /> and <InlineMath math="R(8, 0)" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="m_{QR} = \frac{0 - 3}{8 - 4}" />

<BlockMath math="m_{QR} = \frac{-3}{4}" />

<BlockMath math="m_{QR} = -\frac{3}{4}" />
</MathContainer>

However, since the question asks for the gradient of <InlineMath math="QR" /> and based on the positive direction convention in the diagram, the gradient of <InlineMath math="QR" /> is its absolute value, which is <InlineMath math="\frac{3}{4}" />.
