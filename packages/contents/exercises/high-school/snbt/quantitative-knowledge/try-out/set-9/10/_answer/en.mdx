#### Finding the Side Length Ratio

The volume of a cube (<InlineMath math="V" />) is directly proportional to the cube of its side length (<InlineMath math="s" />). Given that the volume ratio is <InlineMath math="1 : 27" />.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\frac{V_1}{V_2} = \left(\frac{s_1}{s_2}\right)^3" />
  <BlockMath math="\frac{1}{27} = \left(\frac{s_1}{s_2}\right)^3" />
  <BlockMath math="\frac{s_1}{s_2} = \sqrt[3]{\frac{1}{27}}" />
  <BlockMath math="\frac{s_1}{s_2} = \frac{1}{3}" />
</MathContainer>

So, the ratio of the side lengths of the two cubes is <InlineMath math="1 : 3" />.

#### Finding the Surface Area Ratio

The surface area of a cube (<InlineMath math="L" />) is directly proportional to the square of its side length.

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\frac{L_1}{L_2} = \frac{6s_1^2}{6s_2^2}" />
  <BlockMath math="\frac{L_1}{L_2} = \left(\frac{s_1}{s_2}\right)^2" />
  <BlockMath math="\frac{L_1}{L_2} = \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^2" />
  <BlockMath math="\frac{L_1}{L_2} = \frac{1}{9}" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the ratio of the surface areas of the two cubes is <InlineMath math="1 : 9" />.
