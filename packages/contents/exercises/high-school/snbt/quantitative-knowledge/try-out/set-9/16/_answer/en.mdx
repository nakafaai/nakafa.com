Let <InlineMath math="x = 3.15931593..." />.

Since there are <InlineMath math="4" /> repeating digits (which are <InlineMath math="1593" />), we multiply <InlineMath math="x" /> by <InlineMath math="10000" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="10000x = 31593.15931593..." />
</MathContainer>

Next, we subtract the original equation <InlineMath math="x" /> from this equation:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\begin{aligned} 10000x &= 31593.1593... \\ x &= \phantom{3159}3.1593... \\ \hline 9999x &= 31590 \end{aligned}" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, we obtain:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="x = \frac{31590}{9999}" />
</MathContainer>

We simplify the fraction by dividing both the numerator and the denominator by <InlineMath math="9" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="x = \frac{31590 \div 9}{9999 \div 9} = \frac{3510}{1111}" />
</MathContainer>
