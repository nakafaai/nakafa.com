Given the volume of the rectangular prism is <InlineMath math="V = 12x^2" />, the length is <InlineMath math="p = 3x" />, and the width is <InlineMath math="l = 2x" />.

The formula for the volume of a rectangular prism is:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="V = p \times l \times t" />
</MathContainer>

We substitute the known values into the formula:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\begin{aligned} 12x^2 &= (3x) \times (2x) \times t \\ 12x^2 &= 6x^2 \times t \end{aligned}" />
</MathContainer>

Next, we solve for <InlineMath math="t" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="t = \frac{12x^2}{6x^2} = 2" />
</MathContainer>

So, the height of the rectangular prism is <InlineMath math="2" />.
