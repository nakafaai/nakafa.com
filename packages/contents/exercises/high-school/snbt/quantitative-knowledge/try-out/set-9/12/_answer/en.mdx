This problem can be solved using inverse proportion.

Given:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="12 \text{ people} \rightarrow 15 \text{ days}" />
  <BlockMath math="x \text{ people} \rightarrow 9 \text{ days}" />
</MathContainer>

#### Calculating the Number of Workers Needed

Thus, we can write:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\frac{12}{x} = \frac{9}{15}" />
  <BlockMath math="x = \frac{12 \cdot 15}{9}" />
  <BlockMath math="x = 20" />
</MathContainer>

So, the number of workers needed to finish in <InlineMath math="9" /> days is <InlineMath math="20" /> workers.

#### Calculating the Percentage Increase

Thus, the percentage increase in workers is:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\frac{20 - 12}{12} \times 100\% = \frac{8}{12} \times 100\% = 66.7\%" />
</MathContainer>
