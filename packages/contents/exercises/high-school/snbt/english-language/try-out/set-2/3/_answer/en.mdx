This question asks for the author's attitude toward the topic of the passage.

The text is an informative report regarding a new study on the health benefits of swimming. The author presents facts from the report ("The report shows...", "The report also found evidence...") and quotes from relevant officials objectively, without inserting strong personal opinions or emotions.

Therefore, the author's attitude is **Neutral** (objective).

Analysis of other options:

*   **Placid**: Irrelevant to the reporting style.
*   **Exaggerated**: The text presents study findings proportionally, without exaggeration.
*   **Convincing**: While the content is compelling, the primary goal is to inform, not to persuade.
*   **Optimistic**: Although the study findings are positive, the author is simply reporting them neutrally, not expressing personal optimism.
