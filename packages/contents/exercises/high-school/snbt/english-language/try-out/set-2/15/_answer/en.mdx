The author's likely motive is to highlight that **"Some employers haven't yet been open-minded about types of offices supporting their workers' comfort."**

By meticulously contrasting the pros and cons of open versus closed offices, the author illustrates that neither layout is a perfect, one-size-fits-all solution. Open offices boost collaboration but kill concentration; closed offices aid focus but may isolate employees. The underlying message is that employers often adopt trends (like open offices) without fully considering if that environment actually supports their specific employees' needs and comfort. The passage serves as a critique of rigid office designs and suggests a need for more flexibility and open-mindedness from employers.
