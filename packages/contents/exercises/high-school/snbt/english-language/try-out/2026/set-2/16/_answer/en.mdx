The word *cubicles* is closest in meaning to **"enclosed site"**.

In the context of the sentence *"Because your employees are in their own private offices or cubicles,"* the term *cubicles* refers to small, partitioned workspaces that provide privacy and separation, similar to private offices but typically smaller and within a larger room. This aligns perfectly with the definition of an *enclosed site*—a space that is shut in or surrounded by barriers.

Why other options are incorrect:
- **Corral** refers to an enclosure for livestock, which is inappropriate for an office setting.
- **Cuddies** usually refers to small cabins on a ship or small cupboards, not office workspaces.
- **Appartment** (apartment) is a residential unit, not a workspace within an office.
- **Huge rooms** contradicts the nature of a cubicle, which is inherently a small, partitioned area.
