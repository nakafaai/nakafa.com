The topic of the passage is **"Open offices encourage workers to have better communication and teamwork."**

The passage begins by defining open offices and explicitly stating their primary benefit: fostering collaboration, communication, and teamwork. While the text subsequently balances this by discussing the downsides (noise, distraction) and comparing them to closed offices, the central theme remains anchored on the concept and intended purpose of the open office layout.

Why other options are incorrect:
- **Closed offices** are actually stated to *increase* concentration by minimizing distraction, not decrease it.
- The text argues that open offices can *hinder* productivity due to noise, so claiming both layouts *encourage* productivity contradicts the author's points.
- The need for a "quiet room" is presented as a specific requirement for concentration that open offices fail to provide, not a shared feature of both.
- Supervision difficulties are linked specifically to *closed* offices, whereas open plans make supervision easier.
