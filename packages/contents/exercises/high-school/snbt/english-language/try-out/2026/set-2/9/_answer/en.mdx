This question asks us to identify the statement that is **NOT** stated in the text (*except*).

The incorrect statement (not supported by the text) is that immune system diseases are more dangerous than pathogens. The text mentions that a complex immune system comes with "costs" like autoimmune diseases, but it never directly compares the danger level between autoimmune diseases and pathogens.

Analysis of options:

*   **Immune system diseases are more dangerous than pathogens**: Correct as the answer (because it is factually unsupported by the text). The text only mentions autoimmunity as a *cost* or risk of a complex immune system, not that it is *more dangerous* than pathogens themselves.
*   **The migratory birds have to cope with pathogens in two regions**: Stated in the text. Mentioned in paragraph 3 ("migratory birds don't only need to resist diseases in Europe, but also during their migration and in the tropics").
*   **Chronic inflammation is an example of the cost associated to advanced immune system**: Stated in the text. Mentioned in paragraph 4 ("Examples of these costs could be autoimmunity and other immune system diseases such as chronic inflammation").
*   **The immune system of the ancestors of migratory birds was more advanced than that of the migratory birds**: Stated in the text. Their ancestors are African sedentary birds, which have a more varied and extensive immune system (paragraph 2 & 5).
*   **Migratory birds raise their litters in a region with fewer diseases**: Stated in the text. Mentioned in paragraph 1 ("migratory species benefit from leaving tropical areas when it is time to raise their young -- as moving away from diseases").
