This question asks for the sentence closest in meaning to: *"Ocean and coastal ecosystems bring a range of benefits to people worldwide."*

Let's analyze the options:

*   **people in most countries benefit from marine business**: Too specific to "business", whereas the original text discusses ecosystem benefits more broadly (food, livelihood, culture).
*   **oceans benefit the world with water as the main source of rainfalls**: Irrelevant to the context of direct benefits to people mentioned in the text.
*   **many people gain considerable advantages from marine ecosystems**: This is an accurate restatement. "Many people" corresponds to "people worldwide", "gain considerable advantages" corresponds to "bring a range of benefits", and "marine ecosystems" covers "ocean and coastal ecosystems".
*   **explorations from coast to ocean should be done to gain the most benefit**: This is a suggestion/opinion, not a restatement of the fact in the original sentence.
*   **those benefitted by marine lives range from local to global people**: The focus here shifts to *who* is benefited rather than the *action* of the ecosystems providing benefits.

Therefore, the most appropriate choice is the sentence stating that many people gain considerable advantages from marine ecosystems.
