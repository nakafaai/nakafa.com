A new paragraph must have a relationship (*coherence*) and continuity with the previous paragraph so that the discourse remains intact and does not deviate from the main topic.

The last paragraph of the reading text (sentences <InlineMath math="15" /> to <InlineMath math="17" />) concludes that Indonesia remains a significant source of marine pollution in Seychelles and the western Indian Ocean. It mentions physical findings (flip-flops, plastic packaging) and states that this has been identified for a decade.

However, this information is still general and in the form of "suspicion" or "identification". To strengthen this argument in the next paragraph, the most logical thing to discuss is **further explanation regarding Indonesia as the main source of pollution in Seychelles** (*penjelasan lebih lanjut terkait Indonesia sumber utama polusi di Seychelles*).

This topic would provide elaboration, specific data, or mechanisms explaining *why* and *how* Indonesia became that main source in more depth, continuing the premise built in the last paragraph.
