This question asks us to determine the meaning of the word *"berkubang"* according to its context in the text.

Literally (according to KBBI), *"berkubang"* means to wallow or roll in muddy water. However, in the context of sentence <InlineMath math="(7)" /> which reads *"Sebelum Anda berkubang lagi dengan kemacetan jalan yang disesaki polusi"* (Before you wallow again in the traffic jams filled with pollution), this word is used figuratively (connotatively).

In that sentence, *"berkubang dengan kemacetan"* describes a situation where someone is in the middle of traffic congestion that is difficult to avoid and troublesome. The most appropriate meaning to replace *"berkubang"* in this context is **"terjebak"** (trapped/stuck).

So, the sentence can be interpreted as "Before you get **stuck** (*terjebak*) again in the traffic jams...".
