In the Great Dictionary of the Indonesian Language (KBBI) and demographic context, the term "demographic bonus" (*bonus demografi*) refers to a state or condition where the number of people in the productive age group (ranging from 15 to 64 years old) is larger than the non-productive population.

In the context of sentence <InlineMath math="(8)" /> in the text, "demographic bonus" is connected to the "very large population" (*jumlah penduduk yang sangat besar*) mentioned in the previous sentence. This implies that Indonesia has great potential because there are many people in the working age or productive age (*berusia produktif*).

Other options are incorrect because:
- **Strategic geographical location of Indonesia** (*Letak geografis Indonesia yang strategis*): Relates to geography, not demography.
- **Superiority of Indonesian agricultural products** (*Keunggulan produk pertanian Indonesia*): Relates to agrarian economy.
- **Indonesia becoming the number one exporter in the world** (*Indonesia menjadi pengekspor nomor satu dunia*): Is an economic impact, not the definition of demographic bonus itself.
- **Increase in Indonesia's productivity** (*Peningkatan produktivitas Indonesia*): Is the expected result of a demographic bonus, not its definition.
