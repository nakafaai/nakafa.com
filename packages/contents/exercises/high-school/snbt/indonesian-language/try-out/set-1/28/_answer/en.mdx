The word **sekutu** in the Indonesian dictionary (KBBI) means an ally, partner, or countries joined in a union (especially in the context of war). This word has a meaning equivalent to **serikat**, which means an association or union of several parties.

In the history of World War II, "Sekutu" (Allies) refers to the alliance or union of nations (such as the United States, Britain, the Soviet Union, etc.) united against the Japanese Empire and other Axis powers.

Therefore, the most appropriate meaning of the word "sekutu" in the context of the sentence is **Serikat** (union or alliance).
