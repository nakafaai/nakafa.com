To identify a factual sentence, we need to find a sentence that contains accurate, objective data that can be proven true.

Let's look at the options. Sentence <InlineMath math="(7)" /> is merely a general proverb, not specific data. Sentence <InlineMath math="(12)" /> expresses an opinion or suggestion ("Sudah seharusnya..." / "It should be..."), while sentence <InlineMath math="(29)" /> is a subjective assessment ("dinilai efektif" / "considered effective"). Sentence <InlineMath math="(32)" /> is clearly an estimation ("mengira-ngira" / "guessing").

On the other hand, consider sentence <InlineMath math="(2)" />:

> "Berdasarkan laporan Kementerian Lingkungan Hidup dan Kehutanan (KLHK), jumlah gajah sumatra di habitat aslinya di Provinsi Riau tersisa 200 hingga 300 ekor."

This sentence fulfills all the characteristics of a fact: it comes from a trusted source (KLHK report), contains specific quantitative data (200-300 elephants in Riau), and its truth can be verified through the report.

So, the correct answer is <InlineMath math="(2)" />.
