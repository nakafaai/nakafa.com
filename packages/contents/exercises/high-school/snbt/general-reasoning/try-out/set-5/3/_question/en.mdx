Stunting is a condition of growth failure in children due to malnutrition for a long period. As a result, the child is shorter or has a short stature compared to normal children of the same age and has cognitive delays. It is generally caused by food intake that does not meet nutritional needs. Environmental factors that play a role in causing short stature include the mother's nutritional status, insufficient protein in the total proportion of calorie intake, feeding patterns for the child, environmental hygiene, and the incidence of infection in early childhood. In addition to environmental factors, it can also be caused by genetic and hormonal factors.
