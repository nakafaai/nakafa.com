We can solve this problem using a system of linear inequalities.

#### Defining Variables

Let:
- <InlineMath math="F =" /> Father's age
- <InlineMath math="M =" /> Mother's age
- <InlineMath math="C =" /> Child's age

#### Translating Information into Mathematical Model

1. "The difference between Mother's and Father's ages is not more than 5 years":

   <MathContainer>
     <BlockMath math="|F - M| \le 5" />
   </MathContainer>

   This can be written as:

   <MathContainer>
     <BlockMath math="-5 \le F - M \le 5" />
   </MathContainer>

2. "The difference between Father's and their child's age is 35 years":

   <MathContainer>
     <BlockMath math="F - C = 35 \Rightarrow F = C + 35" />
   </MathContainer>

3. "The difference between Mother's and the child's age is x":

   <MathContainer>
     <BlockMath math="x = M - C" />
   </MathContainer>

   (We assume <InlineMath math="M > C" /> since Mother is definitely older than the child).

#### Solving the Inequality

Substitute <InlineMath math="F = C + 35" /> into the inequality:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\begin{aligned} -5 &\le F - M \le 5 \\ -5 &\le (C + 35) - M \le 5 \\ -5 &\le 35 - (M - C) \le 5 \end{aligned}" />
</MathContainer>

Since <InlineMath math="x = M - C" />, the equation becomes:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\begin{aligned} -5 &\le 35 - x \le 5 \end{aligned}" />
</MathContainer>

Subtract <InlineMath math="35" /> from all sides:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\begin{aligned} -5 - 35 &\le -x \le 5 - 35 \\ -40 &\le -x \le -30 \end{aligned}" />
</MathContainer>

Multiply all sides by <InlineMath math="-1" /> (remember, inequality signs reverse):

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\begin{aligned} 40 &\ge x \ge 30 \\ 30 &\le x \le 40 \end{aligned}" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the correct value of <InlineMath math="x" /> is <InlineMath math="30 \le x \le 40" />.
