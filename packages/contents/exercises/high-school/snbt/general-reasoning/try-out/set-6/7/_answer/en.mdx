This problem is an application of **inverse proportion**. Since the distance traveled is constant, if the travel time decreases, the speed must increase.

The relationship between speed (<InlineMath math="v" />) and time (<InlineMath math="t" />) for a constant distance can be written as:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="v_1 \times t_1 = v_2 \times t_2" />
</MathContainer>

Given:
- Initial speed <InlineMath math="v_1 = 60 \text{ km/h}" />
- Initial time <InlineMath math="t_1 = 15 \text{ hours}" />
- Target time <InlineMath math="t_2 = 12 \text{ hours}" />

We substitute these values into the equation to find the new speed (<InlineMath math="v_2" />):

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="60 \times 15 = v_2 \times 12" />
  <BlockMath math="900 = 12 v_2" />
  <BlockMath math="v_2 = \frac{900}{12}" />
  <BlockMath math="v_2 = 75" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the speed of the train must be <InlineMath math="75 \text{ km/h}" />.
