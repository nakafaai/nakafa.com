To solve this problem, we first need to calculate the calorie content of watermelon per <InlineMath math="100" /> grams, and then calculate the total calories for a weight of <InlineMath math="250" /> grams.

#### Calculating Watermelon Calories per 100 grams

It is known that the calorie content of pears per <InlineMath math="100" /> grams is <InlineMath math="57" /> cal.
The calorie content of watermelon is half that of a pear:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Watermelon Calories} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{Pear Calories}" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Watermelon Calories} = \frac{1}{2} \times 57 = 28.5 \text{ cal/100 gr}" />
</MathContainer>

#### Calculating Watermelon Calories for 250 grams

We use direct proportion. If <InlineMath math="100" /> grams of watermelon contains <InlineMath math="28.5" /> calories, then for <InlineMath math="250" /> grams:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total Calories} = \frac{250}{100} \times 28.5" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total Calories} = 2.5 \times 28.5" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total Calories} = 71.25 \text{ cal}" />
</MathContainer>

#### Conclusion

Therefore, the calorie content of <InlineMath math="250" /> gr of watermelon is **<InlineMath math="71.25" /> cal**.
