We can calculate the nutrient content in <InlineMath math="350" /> gr of pear by using a direct proportion relative to the <InlineMath math="100" /> gr baseline.

Given:
*   Pear weight = <InlineMath math="350" /> gr.
*   Baseline = <InlineMath math="100" /> gr.
*   Multiplier factor = <InlineMath math="\frac{350}{100} = 3.5" />.

#### Calculating Carbohydrate

The carbohydrate content per <InlineMath math="100" /> gr is <InlineMath math="15.2" /> gr.
So, for <InlineMath math="350" /> gr:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Carbohydrate} = 3.5 \times 15.2" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Carbohydrate} = 53.2 \text{ gr}" />
</MathContainer>

#### Calculating Fiber

The fiber content per <InlineMath math="100" /> gr is <InlineMath math="3.1" /> gr.
So, for <InlineMath math="350" /> gr:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Fiber} = 3.5 \times 3.1" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Fiber} = 10.85 \text{ gr}" />
</MathContainer>

#### Conclusion

Therefore, the carbohydrate and fiber content are respectively **<InlineMath math="53.2" /> gr and <InlineMath math="10.85" /> gr**.
