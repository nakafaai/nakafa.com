We can solve this problem using direct proportion.

Given:
- Capacity of tool A = <InlineMath math="3 \times" /> capacity of tool B.
- Production of tool B in <InlineMath math="5" /> hours = <InlineMath math="30" /> units.

Steps:
1. Calculate the production of tool A in the same time period (<InlineMath math="5" /> hours). Since A's capacity is <InlineMath math="3" /> times B's, in <InlineMath math="5" /> hours:

   <MathContainer>
     <BlockMath math="A_{5\text{hours}} = 3 \times 30 = 90 \text{ units}" />
   </MathContainer>

2. Calculate the production of tool A in <InlineMath math="2" /> hours. Use the time ratio:

   <MathContainer>
     <BlockMath math="A_{2\text{hours}} = \frac{2}{5} \times 90" />
     <BlockMath math="A_{2\text{hours}} = 2 \times 18" />
     <BlockMath math="A_{2\text{hours}} = 36 \text{ units}" />
   </MathContainer>

Thus, in <InlineMath math="2" /> hours, tool A can produce <InlineMath math="36" /> units.
