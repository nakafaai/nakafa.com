First, we calculate the weight of chocolate powder needed to make <InlineMath math="10\text{ cakes}" /> using the given ratio. The ratio of chocolate to vanilla is <InlineMath math="3 : 2" />, and the weight of vanilla is <InlineMath math="30\text{ grams}" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\frac{\text{Chocolate}}{\text{Vanilla}} = \frac{3}{2}" />
  <BlockMath math="\frac{\text{Chocolate}}{30} = \frac{3}{2}" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Chocolate} = \frac{3 \times 30}{2}" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Chocolate} = 45 \text{ grams}" />
</MathContainer>

Next, we calculate the requirement for <InlineMath math="50\text{ cakes}" />. Since <InlineMath math="50\text{ cakes}" /> is <InlineMath math="5\text{ times}" /> <InlineMath math="10\text{ cakes}" />, the weight of chocolate powder needed is also <InlineMath math="5\text{ times}" /> as much:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total Chocolate} = 45 \times 5" />
  <BlockMath math="\text{Total Chocolate} = 225 \text{ grams}" />
</MathContainer>

So, the weight of chocolate powder needed to make <InlineMath math="50\text{ cakes}" /> is **<InlineMath math="225\text{ grams}" />**.
