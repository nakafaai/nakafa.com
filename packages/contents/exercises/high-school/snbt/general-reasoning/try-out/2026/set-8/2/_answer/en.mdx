To calculate the percentage increase in sales for flower shop B in year <InlineMath math="4" />, we compare the difference in sales between year <InlineMath math="4" /> and year <InlineMath math="3" /> (the previous year), then divide by the sales of year <InlineMath math="3" />.

Based on the chart:
- Sales of flower shop B in year <InlineMath math="3" /> = <InlineMath math="350" />
- Sales of flower shop B in year <InlineMath math="4" /> = <InlineMath math="385" />

The calculation is:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\begin{aligned} \text{Percentage Increase} &= \frac{\text{Sales Yr 4} - \text{Sales Yr 3}}{\text{Sales Yr 3}} \times 100\% \\ &= \frac{385 - 350}{350} \times 100\% \\ &= \frac{35}{350} \times 100\% \\ &= \frac{1}{10} \times 100\% \\ &= 10\% \end{aligned}" />
</MathContainer>

Therefore, the percentage increase in sales for flower shop B in year <InlineMath math="4" /> compared to the previous year is **<InlineMath math="10\%" />**.
