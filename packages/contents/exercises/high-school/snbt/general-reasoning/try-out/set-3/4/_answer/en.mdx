This problem is an example of inverse proportion, where the distance traveled remains constant.

Given:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="v_1 = 60\ \text{km/h}" />
  <BlockMath math="t_1 = 15\ \text{hours}" />
  <BlockMath math="t_2 = 12\ \text{hours}" />
</MathContainer>

We use the distance formula <InlineMath math="S = v \times t" />:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="S_1 = S_2" />
  <BlockMath math="v_1 \times t_1 = v_2 \times t_2" />
  <BlockMath math="60 \times 15 = v_2 \times 12" />
  <BlockMath math="900 = 12 v_2" />
  <BlockMath math="v_2 = \frac{900}{12}" />
  <BlockMath math="v_2 = 75\ \text{km/h}" />
</MathContainer>

Thus, the speed of the train becomes <InlineMath math="75" /> km/h.
