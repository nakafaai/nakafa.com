Given the arithmetic sequence <InlineMath math="5, 8, 11, \ldots" />.

From the sequence:
- First term <InlineMath math="a = 5" />
- Common difference <InlineMath math="b = 8 - 5 = 3" />

Formula for the <InlineMath math="n" />-th term of an arithmetic sequence:

<BlockMath math="U_n = a + (n-1)b" />

Formula for the sum of the first <InlineMath math="n" /> terms:

<BlockMath math="S_n = \frac{n}{2}(2a + (n-1)b)" />

#### Evaluating Statement (1)

Statement: The value of <InlineMath math="a" /> is greater than the value of <InlineMath math="b" />.

<BlockMath math="a = 5 \text{ and } b = 3" />

<BlockMath math="5 > 3" />

Therefore, statement <InlineMath math="(1)" /> is true.

#### Evaluating Statement (2)

Statement: <InlineMath math="U_{11} = a \times 7" />.

Calculate <InlineMath math="U_{11}" />:

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="U_{11} = a + (11-1)b" />

<BlockMath math="= 5 + 10 \times 3" />

<BlockMath math="= 5 + 30" />

<BlockMath math="= 35" />
</MathContainer>

Calculate <InlineMath math="a \times 7" />:

<BlockMath math="a \times 7 = 5 \times 7 = 35" />

Since <InlineMath math="U_{11} = 35" /> and <InlineMath math="a \times 7 = 35" />, then <InlineMath math="U_{11} = a \times 7" />.

Therefore, statement <InlineMath math="(2)" /> is true.

#### Evaluating Statement (3)

Statement: <InlineMath math="S_6 = b \times 25" />.

Calculate <InlineMath math="S_6" />:

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="S_6 = \frac{6}{2}(2a + (6-1)b)" />

<BlockMath math="= 3(2 \times 5 + 5 \times 3)" />

<BlockMath math="= 3(10 + 15)" />

<BlockMath math="= 3 \times 25" />

<BlockMath math="= 75" />
</MathContainer>

Calculate <InlineMath math="b \times 25" />:

<BlockMath math="b \times 25 = 3 \times 25 = 75" />

Since <InlineMath math="S_6 = 75" /> and <InlineMath math="b \times 25 = 75" />, then <InlineMath math="S_6 = b \times 25" />.

Therefore, statement <InlineMath math="(3)" /> is true.

#### Evaluating Statement (4)

Statement: <InlineMath math="U_{18} = S_5" />.

Calculate <InlineMath math="U_{18}" />:

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="U_{18} = a + (18-1)b" />

<BlockMath math="= 5 + 17 \times 3" />

<BlockMath math="= 5 + 51" />

<BlockMath math="= 56" />
</MathContainer>

Calculate <InlineMath math="S_5" />:

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="S_5 = \frac{5}{2}(2a + (5-1)b)" />

<BlockMath math="= \frac{5}{2}(2 \times 5 + 4 \times 3)" />

<BlockMath math="= \frac{5}{2}(10 + 12)" />

<BlockMath math="= \frac{5}{2} \times 22" />

<BlockMath math="= 55" />
</MathContainer>

Since <InlineMath math="U_{18} = 56" /> and <InlineMath math="S_5 = 55" />, then <InlineMath math="U_{18} \neq S_5" />.

Therefore, statement <InlineMath math="(4)" /> is false.
