From the diagram, the coordinates of the rhombus vertices are <InlineMath math="P(0, 0)" />, <InlineMath math="Q(4, 3)" />, <InlineMath math="R(8, 0)" />, and <InlineMath math="S(4, -3)" />.

Calculate the slope of line <InlineMath math="PS" />

<BlockMath math="m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}" />

With <InlineMath math="P(0, 0)" /> and <InlineMath math="S(4, -3)" />

<MathContainer>
<BlockMath math="m_{PS} = \frac{-3 - 0}{4 - 0}" />

<BlockMath math="m_{PS} = \frac{-3}{4}" />

<BlockMath math="m_{PS} = -\frac{3}{4}" />
</MathContainer>

Since the line we're looking for is perpendicular to <InlineMath math="PS" />, then

<BlockMath math="m_1 \cdot m_2 = -1" />

<BlockMath math="-\frac{3}{4} \cdot m_2 = -1" />

<BlockMath math="m_2 = \frac{-1}{-\frac{3}{4}} = \frac{4}{3}" />

So the slope of the line passing through <InlineMath math="Q" /> and perpendicular to <InlineMath math="PS" /> is <InlineMath math="m = \frac{4}{3}" />.
