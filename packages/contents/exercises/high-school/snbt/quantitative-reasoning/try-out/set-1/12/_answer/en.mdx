Given a regular pyramid <InlineMath math="T.ABCD" /> with <InlineMath math="AB = 4 \text{ cm}" /> and point <InlineMath math="O" /> is the intersection point of lines <InlineMath math="AC" /> and <InlineMath math="BD" />.

The volume of the pyramid can be calculated using the formula:

<BlockMath math="V = \frac{1}{3} \times L_{\text{base}} \times h = \frac{1}{3} \times s^2 \times h" />

where <InlineMath math="s" /> is the side length of the base and <InlineMath math="h" /> is the height of the pyramid.

Since <InlineMath math="AB = 4 \text{ cm}" />, the base area <InlineMath math="L_{\text{base}} = 4^2 = 16 \text{ cm}^2" />.

To calculate the volume, we need to know the height of the pyramid <InlineMath math="h = TO" />.

**Analysis of Statement <InlineMath math="(1)" />:** <InlineMath math="AC = 4\sqrt{2} \text{ cm}" />

The diagonal of a square with side <InlineMath math="s" /> is <InlineMath math="s\sqrt{2}" />.

If <InlineMath math="AC = 4\sqrt{2} \text{ cm}" />, then:

<BlockMath math="s\sqrt{2} = 4\sqrt{2}" />

<BlockMath math="s = 4" />

This is consistent with the given information (<InlineMath math="AB = 4 \text{ cm}" />).

Point <InlineMath math="O" /> is the midpoint of the diagonal, so:

<BlockMath math="AO = OC = \frac{AC}{2} = \frac{4\sqrt{2}}{2} = 2\sqrt{2} \text{ cm}" />

However, statement <InlineMath math="(1)" /> does not provide information about the height of the pyramid <InlineMath math="TO" />. Without the height, the volume cannot be determined.

Therefore, statement <InlineMath math="(1)" /> ALONE is not sufficient.

**Analysis of Statement <InlineMath math="(2)" />:** <InlineMath math="AT = \sqrt{17} \text{ cm}" />

Since <InlineMath math="AB = 4 \text{ cm}" />, the base diagonal <InlineMath math="AC = 4\sqrt{2} \text{ cm}" />.

Point <InlineMath math="O" /> is the midpoint of the diagonal, so:

<BlockMath math="AO = \frac{AC}{2} = \frac{4\sqrt{2}}{2} = 2\sqrt{2} \text{ cm}" />

Consider the right triangle <InlineMath math="AOT" /> with a right angle at <InlineMath math="O" /> (since <InlineMath math="TO" /> is perpendicular to the base).

Using the Pythagorean theorem:

<BlockMath math="AT^2 = AO^2 + TO^2" />

<BlockMath math="(\sqrt{17})^2 = (2\sqrt{2})^2 + TO^2" />

<BlockMath math="17 = 8 + TO^2" />

<BlockMath math="TO^2 = 9" />

<BlockMath math="TO = 3 \text{ cm}" />

With height <InlineMath math="TO = 3 \text{ cm}" /> and base area <InlineMath math="L_{\text{base}} = 16 \text{ cm}^2" />, the volume of the pyramid can be calculated:

<BlockMath math="V = \frac{1}{3} \times 16 \times 3 = 16 \text{ cm}^3" />

Therefore, statement <InlineMath math="(2)" /> ALONE is sufficient to answer the question.
