Given the operation <InlineMath math="\triangle(a, b) = ka + \frac{b}{m}" /> where <InlineMath math="k" /> and <InlineMath math="m" /> are positive integers.

From <InlineMath math="\triangle(2, 4) = 8" />, we get

<BlockMath math="\triangle(2, 4) = 2k + \frac{4}{m} = 8" />

<BlockMath math="2km + 4 = 8m \quad \ldots \text{(1)}" />

From <InlineMath math="\triangle(3, 5) = 11" />, we get

<BlockMath math="\triangle(3, 5) = 3k + \frac{5}{m} = 11" />

Multiply both sides by <InlineMath math="m" />

<BlockMath math="3km + 5 = 11m \quad \ldots \text{(2)}" />

Eliminate <InlineMath math="km" /> by multiplying equation <InlineMath math="\text{(1)}" /> by <InlineMath math="3" /> and equation <InlineMath math="\text{(2)}" /> by <InlineMath math="2" />

<MathContainer>

<BlockMath math="6km + 10 = 22m \quad \ldots \text{(2a)}" />
</MathContainer>

Subtract equation <InlineMath math="\text{(2a)}" /> from equation <InlineMath math="\text{(1a)}" />

<BlockMath math="\text{(1a)} - \text{(2a)}: \quad (6km + 12) - (6km + 10) = 24m - 22m" />

<BlockMath math="2 = 2m" />

<BlockMath math="m = 1" />

Substitute <InlineMath math="m = 1" /> into equation <InlineMath math="\text{(1)}" />

<BlockMath math="2k(1) + 4 = 8(1)" />

<BlockMath math="2k + 4 = 8" />

<BlockMath math="2k = 4" />
