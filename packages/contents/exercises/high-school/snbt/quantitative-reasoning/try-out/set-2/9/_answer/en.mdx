Given the matrix equation:

<BlockMath math="A \times M = B" />

Where <InlineMath math="A = \begin{pmatrix} 6 & 3 \\ b & 14 \end{pmatrix}" /> and <InlineMath math="B = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 2 \\ 4 & 5 \end{pmatrix}" />.

If both sides are multiplied by <InlineMath math="M^{-1}" /> from the right, then:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="A \times M \times M^{-1} = B \times M^{-1}" />
  <BlockMath math="A \times I = B \times M^{-1}" />
  <BlockMath math="A = B \times M^{-1}" />
</MathContainer>

Substitute the known matrices:

<BlockMath math="\begin{pmatrix} 6 & 3 \\ b & 14 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 2 \\ 4 & 5 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} a & 1 \\ 3 & 2 \end{pmatrix}" />

Perform matrix multiplication on the right side:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\begin{pmatrix} 6 & 3 \\ b & 14 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} (-1)(a) + (2)(3) & (-1)(1) + (2)(2) \\ (4)(a) + (5)(3) & (4)(1) + (5)(2) \end{pmatrix}" />
  <BlockMath math="\begin{pmatrix} 6 & 3 \\ b & 14 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -a + 6 & -1 + 4 \\ 4a + 15 & 4 + 10 \end{pmatrix}" />
  <BlockMath math="\begin{pmatrix} 6 & 3 \\ b & 14 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -a + 6 & 3 \\ 4a + 15 & 14 \end{pmatrix}" />
</MathContainer>

From the equality of two matrices, we obtain:

**Element at row 1 column 1:**

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="6 = -a + 6" />
  <BlockMath math="a = 0" />
</MathContainer>

**Element at row 2 column 1:**

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="b = 4a + 15" />
  <BlockMath math="b = 4(0) + 15" />
  <BlockMath math="b = 15" />
</MathContainer>

Now we check the truth of each statement.

#### Statement 1

States that <InlineMath math="a = 2" />.
Since the calculated value is <InlineMath math="a = 0" />, this statement is **False**.

#### Statement 2

States that <InlineMath math="a + b = 15" />.
Calculate <InlineMath math="a + b" />:

<BlockMath math="a + b = 0 + 15 = 15" />

This statement is **True**.

#### Statement 3

States that the determinant of the inverse matrix <InlineMath math="M" /> is <InlineMath math="3" />.
The inverse matrix <InlineMath math="M" /> is <InlineMath math="M^{-1} = \begin{pmatrix} a & 1 \\ 3 & 2 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 3 & 2 \end{pmatrix}" />.

The determinant of <InlineMath math="M^{-1}" /> is:

<MathContainer>
  <BlockMath math="\det(M^{-1}) = (0)(2) - (1)(3)" />
  <BlockMath math="\det(M^{-1}) = 0 - 3 = -3" />
</MathContainer>

Since the determinant result is <InlineMath math="-3" />, not <InlineMath math="3" />, this statement is **False**.

#### Conclusion

The correct sequence of truth values is **False, True, False**.
