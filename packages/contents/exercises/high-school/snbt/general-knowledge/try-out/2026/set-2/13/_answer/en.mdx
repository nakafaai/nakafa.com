To find a sentence with the same pattern, we need to break down the structure of sentence <InlineMath math="(4)" /> first. Its pattern is Adjunct - Subject - Predicate - Complement (A-S-P-C).

- Adjunct: *If that happens,*
- Subject: *the first thing to do*
- Predicate: *is*
- Complement: *face it calmly and do not panic.*

The sentence that has the exact same structural pattern as above is "When compared to other high-protein foods, such as meat, eggs are a relatively affordable source of protein."

- Adjunct: *When compared to other high-protein foods, such as meat,*
- Subject: *eggs*
- Predicate: *are*
- Complement: *a relatively affordable source of protein.*
