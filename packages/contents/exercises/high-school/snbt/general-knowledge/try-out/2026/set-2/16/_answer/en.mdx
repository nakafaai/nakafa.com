Changing a word to a more polite or refined synonym is known as semantic amelioration or euphemism.

In the text, the phrase deliberately changed by the author to sound softer is *his body was thinning*. The use of the word *thinning* in that phrase is a refined form used to describe a person's physical condition, where the original intended meaning is *his body was getting skinny*.
