A group of words (phrase) often has a figurative or idiomatic meaning that cannot be interpreted literally word for word.

In the text, specifically in sentence <InlineMath math="(3)" />, the phrase *spoke up* is used to describe dr. Michael Triangto responding to various sports issues in the community.

The most appropriate meaning of the phrase *spoke up* in this context is to start talking to express an opinion, state a stance, or **providing an explanation or response** to an issue that is currently circulating.

Other choices, such as merely *talking about something* or *discussing something*, have meanings that are too general, while *denying* or *bargaining* do not fit the context of the sentence where the doctor aims to straighten out the confusing information.
