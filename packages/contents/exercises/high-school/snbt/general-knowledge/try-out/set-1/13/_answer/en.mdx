The main idea of a sentence is the primary information consisting basically of the Subject (S) and Predicate (P), without including any explanatory elements or other extensions.

If we break down sentence <InlineMath math="(2)" /> ("They were fascinated by the beauty of the expanse of grass covered in clear ice crystals."), its structure consists of the Subject (They), Predicate (were fascinated), and Explanatory element (by the beauty of the expanse of grass covered in clear ice crystals). Because the part after the phrase "were fascinated" is merely a complementary explanatory element, the main information or the core of the sentence is "They were fascinated."
