## Understanding Reflection over the Y-axis

Reflection over the Y-axis is a type of geometric transformation that moves every point on an object to a new position. Imagine the Y-axis as a mirror. Every point will have an image on the opposite side of the Y-axis at the same distance from the Y-axis.

### Rule for Reflection over the Y-axis

If a point <InlineMath math="P(x, y)" /> is reflected over the Y-axis, its image's coordinates, <InlineMath math="P'(x', y')" />, will follow the rule:

<div className="flex flex-col gap-4">
  <BlockMath math="x' = -x" />
  <BlockMath math="y' = y" />
</div>

Thus, the image of point <InlineMath math="P(x, y)" /> is <InlineMath math="P'(-x, y)" />.

Note that the value of the y-coordinate does not change, while the value of the x-coordinate becomes its opposite (negative if positive, positive if negative).

## Reflecting a Point

Suppose we have point <InlineMath math="A(3, 4)" />. If point A is reflected over the Y-axis, its image, <InlineMath math="A'" />, can be determined as follows:

The original x-coordinate is 3, so <InlineMath math="x' = -3" />.

The original y-coordinate is 4, so <InlineMath math="y' = 4" />.

Thus, the image of point A is <InlineMath math="A'(-3, 4)" />.

## Reflecting a Triangle

Now, let's reflect a triangle <InlineMath math="PQR" /> with vertices <InlineMath math="P(1, 2)" />, <InlineMath math="Q(4, 4)" />, and <InlineMath math="R(2, 0)" /> over the Y-axis.

To reflect a triangle, we need to reflect each of its vertices.

1.  Point <InlineMath math="P(1, 2)" />: Its image is <InlineMath math="P'(-1, 2)" />.
2.  Point <InlineMath math="Q(4, 4)" />: Its image is <InlineMath math="Q'(-4, 4)" />.
3.  Point <InlineMath math="R(2, 0)" />: Its image is <InlineMath math="R'(-2, 0)" />.

By connecting the image points <InlineMath math="P'Q'R'" />, we obtain the reflected triangle.

## Reflecting a Line Equation

Suppose we have a line with the equation <InlineMath math="y = x + 2" />. To find the equation of its image after reflection over the Y-axis, we substitute <InlineMath math="x" /> with <InlineMath math="-x" /> (because <InlineMath math="x' = -x" />) and <InlineMath math="y" /> with <InlineMath math="y" /> (because <InlineMath math="y' = y" />) into the original equation.

Original equation:

<BlockMath math="y = x + 2" />

Substitute <InlineMath math="x \rightarrow -x" />:

<BlockMath math="y = (-x) + 2" />

The equation of the image is:

<BlockMath math="y = -x + 2" />

Let's visualize these two lines:

## Exercises

1.  Determine the coordinates of the image of point <InlineMath math="K(-5, 8)" /> if it is reflected over the Y-axis!
2.  A triangle <InlineMath math="ABC" /> has vertices <InlineMath math="A(2, 1)" />, <InlineMath math="B(5, 3)" />, and <InlineMath math="C(3, 6)" />. Determine the coordinates of the image triangle <InlineMath math="A'B'C'" /> after reflection over the Y-axis!
3.  Determine the equation of the image of the line <InlineMath math="3x - 2y + 6 = 0" /> if it is reflected over the Y-axis!
4.  A line has the equation <InlineMath math="y = -3x - 4" />. Determine the equation of its image after reflection over the Y-axis.

### Key Answers

1.  The image of point <InlineMath math="K(-5, 8)" /> is <InlineMath math="K'(5, 8)" />.

    **Explanation:** <InlineMath math="x' = -(-5) = 5" />, <InlineMath math="y' = 8" />.

2.  The coordinates of the image triangle <InlineMath math="A'B'C'" /> are:

    - <InlineMath math="A'(-2, 1)" />
    - <InlineMath math="B'(-5, 3)" />
    - <InlineMath math="C'(-3, 6)" />

3.  The equation of the image of the line <InlineMath math="3x - 2y + 6 = 0" /> is <InlineMath math="-3x - 2y + 6 = 0" />.

    **Explanation:** Substitute <InlineMath math="x" /> with <InlineMath math="-x" /> into the original equation:

    <div className="flex flex-col gap-4">
      <BlockMath math="3(-x) - 2y + 6 = 0" />
      <BlockMath math="-3x - 2y + 6 = 0" />
    </div>

4.  The equation of the image of the line <InlineMath math="y = -3x - 4" /> is <InlineMath math="y = 3x - 4" />.

    **Explanation:** Substitute <InlineMath math="x" /> with <InlineMath math="-x" />:

    <div className="flex flex-col gap-4">
      <BlockMath math="y = -3(-x) - 4" />
      <BlockMath math="y = 3x - 4" />
    </div>
